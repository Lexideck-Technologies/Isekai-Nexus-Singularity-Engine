# ISEKAI WORLD: NECRO-FLORA PRIME

## World Classification // Revised v4.2

**World Type:** [A Rank] - [Bio-Gothic / Aristocratic Horror / Darwinian Ecosystem]
**Danger Level:** **Extreme.** The environment is actively predatory; the social structure is aggressively lethal.
**Power Ceiling:** **National/Continental.** (Grand Voivodes can reshape cities or forest biomes, but cannot destroy the planet).

## World Overview

Necro-Flora Prime is a testament to evolution gone wrong—or perhaps, too right. Here, the distinction between fauna and flora has collapsed. The ecosystem is a hyper-carnivorous garden where "survival of the fittest" is not a theory, but a visible, bloody minute-by-minute struggle. The only sanctuaries are the **Ossuary Cities**—colossal gothic metropolises carved directly into the fossilized skeletons of antediluvian mega-beasts (Titan-class entities that died eons ago).

The ruling class, the **Voivodate**, are immortal flesh-crafters who view their subjects as clay. They practice a twisted nobility, sipping vintage fear-wine in ballrooms grown from living ivory. Technology is strictly biological: bone-crafting, blood-hydraulic engines, and nerve-wire circuitry. To be beautiful here is to be dangerous; to be ugly is to be food.

**Climate/Environment:** Perpetual Twilight. The sky is choked by spore-clouds. The continent is surrounded by the **Red-Green Sea**, a viscous slurry of algae and hemoglobin.

## Starting Scenarios

[Survival scenario - Awakening in a holding pen]

[Educational scenario - Inducted into a Voivode's household]

[Professional scenario - Taming the wild]

* **The Pruners:** Defensive specialists who maintain the "Kill Zones" around cities.
* **The Harvesters:** Special operations teams that retrieve luxury poisonous flowers for the nobility.

**First Missions:** "Weed Control" - Burn back an encroachment of Strangle-Vines threatening to crush a slum district.

### D) The Party - "The Resistance"

[Pre-existing group scenario - Rebels against the Voivodate]

**Starting Kit:** Guerrilla fatigues, stolen bone-weapons, 3 days of rations.
**Party Composition:**

* **Gargoyle (Tank):** An escaped "War-Ghoul" who regained free will; heavy bone plating.
* **Hemomancer (DPS):** A rogue scholar who uses forbidden blood magic to boil enemies.
* **The "Pure" (Face):** An unmodified human whose rarity grants them high charisma and diplomatic value.
**Initial Mission:** Ambush a Vitae-Caravan to steal supplies without alerting the aerial scouts (Giant Bat-Moths).

### E) The Deep End - "The Cathedral of Skin"

[Horror scenario - High-risk servitude]

**Starting Kit:** **[Debuff: Modified]** (e.g., mouth sewn shut or hands fused into serving trays). No items.
**Immediate Threats:** You serve a High Lord during the "Red Feast." Spilling wine or making eye contact results in immediate execution.
**Survival Rewards:** Surviving the feast and reversing your modification grants **[Trait: Flesh-Mastery]** (Control over your own biology/regeneration).

### F) The Transplant - "The Foreign Guest"

[High-powered start - Diplomatic Immunity]

**Starting Kit:** Silk finery, **[Trait: Alien Biology]** (Immune to local diseases/poisons), Letter of Introduction.
**Recruiter:** **Voivode Vlado**, a bored aesthetician.
**Purpose:** Vlado finds your "Earth physiology" fascinatingly symmetrical and static. He wants you to teach him Earth culture. Fail to entertain him, and you become a specimen.

### G) The Regression - "The Ancient One"

[Veteran scenario - Awakening from Torpor]

**Regression Path (Awakening):** You were a High Voivode who slept for 1,000 years.
**Starting Kit:** **S-Tier Bloodline** (Dormant), but your castle is a ruin and your descendants believe you are a myth.
**Impending Disaster:** The **Gehenna Bloom**—a dormant planetary parasite—is waking up, and only you remember how to stop it.

## Geography & Major Locations

### The Rib-Cage Valley

* **The Ossuary City of Sternum:** The capital. Built inside the chest cavity of a dead leviathan. The rich live in the "Spine" (high altitude), the poor live in the "Pelvis" (the slums).
* **The Red-Green Sea:** The toxic ocean. Ships here are grown from whales and navigate through semi-solid algal mats.

### The Wilderness

* **The Whispering Weald:** A forest where the trees have vocal cords. They mimic the voices of loved ones to lure travelers into their roots.
* **The Vein-Rivers:** Rivers of thick, oxygen-rich fluid that pulse rhythmically. Drinking it causes mutation.

## Factions & Politics

### Major Factions

**The Sanguine Court (Lawful Evil):**

* *Philosophy:* Perfection through modification. The strong shape the weak.
* *Methods:* Bio-thaumaturgy, political marriages, assassination.
* *Leadership:* The Council of Seven (The Grand Voivodes).
* *Reputation Effects:* Hated by the poor, feared by all.

**The Root-Bound (Chaotic Good/Neutral):**

* *Philosophy:* Return to nature. Reject the "flesh-crafting" of the cities.
* *Methods:* Druidic magic, guerrilla warfare, using plants against the ghouls.
* *Leadership:* The Matriarch of Thorns.
* *Reputation Effects:* Enemies of the state.

## Power Systems & Magic

### The Visceral Arts (Bio-Thaumaturgy)

**Description:** Magic that allows the user to command organic matter at a cellular level.
**Casting Method:** Somatic (Hand gestures touching flesh) & Alchemical.
**Mana Source:** **Vitae** (Life Force/Blood). Using magic drains your own HP or requires a blood source.
**Skill Requirements:** Anatomy, Willpower, and a lack of empathy.

* **Osteomancy (Bone):** Hardening skin, growing spikes, shaping buildings.
* **Hemomancy (Blood):** Boiling blood, controlling hydraulic pressure, curing diseases.
* **Nerve-Jacking:** Controlling the muscles of others (Puppetry).

### Secondary System: Botanomancy

**Source:** Nature/Spores.
**Users:** The Root-Bound and Guild Gardeners.
**Abilities:** Accelerating plant growth, synthesizing poisons, communing with the forest mind.

## Isekai Integration Elements

### Modern Knowledge Applications

* **Genetics (Mendelian/CRISPR):** The locals craft flesh like art (intuition). A user with knowledge of DNA, recessive genes, and viral vectors can create "plagues" or "cures" with 1000% more efficiency than local magic.
* **Sanitation/Germ Theory:** The world is plagued by "Rot." Introducing sterilization and antibiotics would revolutionize medicine and mortality rates.
* **Agriculture:** Introducing crop rotation and non-carnivorous farming (hydroponics) to break the reliance on the "Hunter Economy."

### Cheat Skill Synergies

* **[Regeneration]:** In a world where flesh is currency, you are a walking gold mine. You can sell your kidney every morning and regrow it by noon.
* **[Purification]:** The ability to instantly clean tainted Vitae or cure "Bone-Rot" makes you a messianic figure to the masses.
* **[Appraisal]:** Essential for identifying which plants will eat you versus which will heal you.

## Signature Quests

### Repeatable Activities

* **The Midnight Ball:** Navigate high-stakes politics. Win favor by presenting the most "beautifully monstrous" pet or creation.
* **Ghoul-Hunting:** Track down failed experiments that have escaped into the sewers before they breed.

### Major Quest Lines

**"The Metamorphosis"**
You discover the planet is an egg. The "Mega-Fauna Skeletons" aren't dead; they are pupating. The Voivodes are trying to wake them up to ride them across the stars.

* *Stakes:* If they wake, the cities built inside them will be crushed.

**"The Cure for Death"**
A quest to find the legendary "White Flower," the only plant on the planet rumored to be non-carnivorous and capable of reversing Ghoulification.

* *Rewards:* The ability to restore humanity to the monsters.

## Unique Features

### Living Architecture

Most buildings are alive. Doors are sphincters that must be tickled or fed to open. Heating is provided by the building's circulation system.

* *Impact:* Houses can get "sick," "infected," or simply decide they don't like you and digest you in your sleep.

### The Red Rain

A weather phenomenon where atmospheric pressure drops and it rains diluted Vitae.

* *Impact:* All plant life enters a frenzy state (Speed +200%). All Flesh-Magic is boosted (Potency +50%).

## Achievement System

### Artistic Merits

* **"Master Sculptor":** Craft a minion judged "Sublime" by a High Voivode.
* **"Iron Stomach":** Survive eating a meal prepared by the nobility without vomiting or dying.

### Ultimate Achievements

* **"The Amaranth":** Consume the soul/essence of a Grand Voivode and take their seat on the Council.
* **"Gardener of Eden":** Terraform a region of the planet into a safe, passive ecosystem (Earth-like).
