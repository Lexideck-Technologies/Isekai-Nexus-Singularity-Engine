# ISEKAI WORLD: IRON-DAWN 2750

## World Classification

**World Type:** [Rank B] - Solar-Corps (Magitech Civilization / Dystopian Wasteland Hybrid)
**Danger Level:** High (Active Interplanetary Civil War / Lawless Frontiers)
**Power Ceiling:** AA-Rank (Experimental "Singularity" Frames & Ace Pilots)

## World Overview

The year is 2750 CE. Humanity has sprawled across the Solar System, but the dream of a unified utopia died in the vacuum of space. The Earth Sphere is a decaying bureaucracy, while the colonies of Mars, the Belt, and the Jovian Moons are a lawless frontier ruled by corporate warlords and crime syndicates. The defining technology of this era is the **Mobile Frame** (or "Frame")—originally designed for heavy industrial labor in high-gravity environments, now retrofitted with crude armor, railguns, and missile pods to become the ultimate weapon of war.

This is the dawn of the Mecha Era. There are no energy shields, no beam sabers, and no flight-capable Gundams. Combat is visceral, heavy, and grounded. Frames leak oil, overheat, and rely on massive ballistic ammunition. Pilots connect to their machines via painful spinal "Neural-Jacks." It is a world of grease, gears, and cold vacuum, where life is cheap and a good mechanic is worth more than a politician.

**Climate/Environment:** Ranges from the claustrophobic, neon-drenched megacities of Earth to the red dust storms of Mars and the absolute zero of the Asteroid Belt. Survival gear is mandatory outside of pressurized zones.

## Geography & Major Locations

### The Inner Sphere (Earth & Luna)

* **The Spire (Earth):** A massive orbital elevator city where the elite live above the smog. The center of political power and corruption.
* **Tycho Shipyards (Luna):** The largest manufacturing hub in the system. Home of the Terran Coalition Navy.

### The Red Zone (Mars)

* **Olympus City:** A domed metropolis controlled by rival corporations. The streets are a cyberpunk nightmare of neon and crime.
* **The Rust Wastes:** The terraforming failed here. Lawless badlands ruled by "Viper" and his raiders.

* **Ceres Station:** The "Tortuga" of space. A hollowed-out asteroid serving as the headquarters for the Black Orbit Syndicate.
* **The Gas Mines (Jupiter Orbit):** Dangerous, high-gravity extraction zones where life expectancy is measured in weeks.

## Power Systems & Magic

### The Neural-Link System

Magic does not exist. Power comes from the **Sync Rate**—the connection between the pilot's nervous system and the Frame's CPU.

**Casting Method:** **"Overclocking."** Pilots can push their Sync Rate beyond safety limits (100%+). This grants bullet-time reflexes and pinpoint accuracy but causes physical brain damage (HP loss) and "Neural Burn" (Stat debuffs).
**Mana Source:** **Reactor Energy (EN).** Frames run on batteries/generators. Moving, shooting, and boosting consumes EN. Running out leaves you a sitting duck.
**Skill Requirements:** **[Piloting]**, **[Engineering]**, **[Mental Fortitude]**.

### Secondary Systems

* **Modding:** Knowledge is power. A mechanic with high **[DEX]** and **[INT]** can tune a crappy engine to outperform a military grade one.
* **Ballistics:** Understanding physics, trajectory, and recoil control is vital.

## Isekai Integration Elements

### Modern Knowledge Applications

* **Engineering:** Understanding modern aerodynamics or composite materials allows for "impossible" upgrades to primitive mechs.
* **Gaming Tactics:** "Kiting," "circle-strafing," and "animation canceling" are unknown concepts here. A gamer pilot moves in ways military doctrine can't predict.
* **Physics:** Calculating orbital mechanics for "slingshot maneuvers" without a computer.

### Cheat Skill Synergies

* **[Machine Affinity]:** In a world of clunky tech, the ability to "feel" the machine allows the user to bypass the Neural-Link pain and achieve 100% Sync instantly.
* **[Inventory]:** Storing spare ammo, repair kits, or even an *entire backup mech* in a pocket dimension is a game-changer in logistical warfare.
* **[Appraisal]:** Instantly seeing enemy weak points (e.g., "Reactor Shielding: 15% Integrity") allows for one-shot kills.

### Romance & Relationship Opportunities

Relationships are forged in fire. "Cockpit comms" confessions and shared quarters on cramped ships are the norm.

* *The Rival:* A enemies-to-lovers arc with an Ace from the opposing faction.
* *The Operator:* The voice in your ear who guides you through the mission.
* *The Heir/Heiress:* A naive corporate scion who needs protection and shows you the soft side of the hard world.

* *Stakes:* If ignored, a robotic invasion (The Grey Goo) will consume all organic life.

## Unique Iron-Dawn Features

### The Scavenger Economy

* **Feature:** Money is scarce. Most upgrades come from **looting** defeated enemies. If you shoot a mech in the legs, you can salvage its pristine arm-weapons. If you blow up the core, you get nothing. Precision matters.

### "Heat" Mechanics

* **Element:** Frames generate heat. Firing weapons and boosting raises the heat gauge. If it maxes out, the Frame shuts down (stunned) or explodes. Managing heat sinks is a core tactical layer.
