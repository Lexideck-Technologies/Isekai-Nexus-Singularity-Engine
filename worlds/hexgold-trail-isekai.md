# ISEKAI WORLD: Hexgold Trail

## World Classification

**World Type:** Alternate Earth - AA-Tier Weird Western Frontier
**Danger Level:** Moderate to High - Human conflicts, natural disasters, unpredictable Resonance forces  
**Power Ceiling:** Ultimate Resonance - Life-changing events, potent spirit conjuration, improbable fortune/misfortune

---

## World Overview

The Hexgold Trail setting plunges players into the gritty and often perilous world of mid-to-late 19th-century American West during the Gold Rush. Three city hubs (San Francisco, Sacramento, and Carson City) serve as the heart of civilization, providing refuge from the harsh conditions of the frontier. Yet, many venture into the rugged wilderness, driven by gold fever and the promise of riches. There are no monsters or aliens here - only human challenges, natural disasters, and the eerie Resonance, a mysterious force that manifests as different forms of luck and hexes.

This 19th century Gold Rush alternate Earth adventure setting stretches from Dodge City to San Francisco, providing a diverse range of landscapes and environments, from bustling frontier towns to rugged wilderness. The specific location of the game can be set anywhere along this trail, depending on character details and the game master's preference. Whether it's the windy plains around Dodge City, the towering Rockies, or the Pacific vistas of San Francisco, each area comes with its unique challenges, opportunities, and Resonance manifestations. This vast and varied setting provides ample room for the game master to create a truly immersive experience.

**Climate/Environment:** Diverse landscapes including windy plains, towering mountains, rugged wilderness, and coastal vistas. Harsh frontier conditions with natural disasters, gold fever-driven conflicts, and enigmatic Resonance phenomena.

## Starting Scenarios

### A) The Classic - "Gold Fever Awakening"

[Lone survivor scenario - awakening in a neutral frontier town after a mysterious incident]

**Starting Kit:** Basic prospecting tools, revolver, trail clothes, small stake of gold dust  
**Initial Challenges:** Navigating frontier culture, avoiding outlaws, dealing with Resonance hexes

[Educational institution scenario - military outpost or pioneer training camp]

**Starting Kit:** Uniform, basic weapons training, small allowance for supplies  
**Initial State:** New recruit status

**Social Dynamics:** Fellow recruits, stern instructors, rival trainees

### C) The Guild - "Prospector's Union"

[Professional organization scenario - prospector's guild or mining cooperative]

**Starting Kit:** Mining gear, claim stake, guild membership badge  
**Guild Options:** Gold Diggers cooperative, Railroad surveyors, Native trade alliances  
**Initial Reputation:** Probationary member  
**First Missions:** Assist in gold hunts, protect claims, scout new veins

### D) The Party - "Trailblazing Posse"

[Pre-existing group scenario - already integrated into a posse or wagon train]

**Starting Kit:** Role-appropriate gear (scout rifle, medical kit, etc.)  
**Party Composition:** Veteran prospector, sharp-shooting pioneer, mystical shaman, cunning outlaw  
**Initial Mission:** Escort wagon train through dangerous territory

### E) The Deep End - "Resonance Storm Survivor"

[High-risk scenario - caught in a Resonance event or outlaw ambush]

**Starting Kit:** Damaged gear, minimal supplies, unexplained Resonance mark  
**Immediate Threats:** Pursuing outlaws, environmental hazards, Resonance curses  
**Survival Rewards:** Enhanced Resonance sensitivity, outlaw contacts, survival skills

### F) The Transplant - "Divine Frontier Calling"

[Divine intervention scenario - summoned or transplanted by a divine entity to fulfill a frontier prophecy]

**Starting Kit:** Blessed prospecting tools, holy relic, divine guidance visions, small divine favor  
**Initial Challenges:** Interpreting divine visions, balancing faith with frontier survival, evading those who seek the relic  
**First Contact:** Mysterious preacher or divine messenger in a frontier church

[Regression scenario - awakening with memories of past lives on similar frontiers, granting wisdom but inner conflict]

**Starting Kit:** Familiar gear from past lives, journal of memories, uncanny déjà vu abilities  
**Initial Challenges:** Integrating past life knowledge, dealing with anachronistic skills, confronting personal regrets  
**First Contact:** Old acquaintance from a past life or a Resonance echo  
**Past Life Options:**

- **Western Pioneer:** Memories of 19th-century American West, enhancing survival and marksmanship skills.  
- **Gold Rush Survivor:** Recollections of historical gold rushes, improving prospecting and negotiation abilities.

## Geography & Major Locations

### Western Trail Hubs

- **San Francisco:** Bustling port city, heart of civilization, railroad terminus, hub for merchants and adventurers
- **Sacramento:** River city, government center, supply depot for prospectors, site of major gold discoveries
- **Carson City:** Mountain hub, mining town, frontier law enforcement base, gateway to uncharted territories

### Key Regions

- **Dodge City Plains:** Windy grasslands, outlaw hideouts, cattle drives, minor Resonance luck shifts
- **Rocky Mountains:** Towering peaks, treacherous passes, hidden gold veins, advanced Resonance manifestations
- **Pacific Coast:** Coastal trails, shipwrecks, diverse wildlife, ultimate Resonance events

## Factions & Politics

### Major Factions

**Gold Diggers (Neutral-Chaotic):**

- *Philosophy:* Live and die by the gold they find, prioritize personal fortune over laws or ethics
- *Methods:* Prospecting, claim jumping, gang warfare, Resonance exploitation
- *Leadership:* Loose coalitions of gang leaders and wealthy prospectors
- *Reputation Effects:* Trusted by fellow diggers, distrusted by lawmen and natives

**Frontier Lawmen (Lawful-Good):**

- *Philosophy:* Impose order in chaotic frontier, protect settlers and uphold justice
- *Methods:* Organize posses, bounty hunting, town defense, legal enforcement
- *Leadership:* Sheriffs, marshals, and federal agents
- *Reputation Effects:* Respected by townsfolk, feared by outlaws

**Railroad Companies (Neutral-Evil):**

- *Philosophy:* Connect frontier to civilization through infrastructure, profit from expansion
- *Methods:* Land grabs, labor exploitation, sabotage of competitors, political corruption
- *Leadership:* Corporate executives and ruthless foremen
- *Reputation Effects:* Welcomed by merchants, resented by natives and small prospectors

**Native Tribes (Chaotic-Neutral):**

- *Philosophy:* Defend ancestral lands and traditions against encroaching invaders
- *Methods:* Guerrilla warfare, spiritual resistance, trade alliances, Resonance communion
- *Leadership:* Tribal elders and shamans
- *Reputation Effects:* Feared by settlers, respected by those showing cultural sensitivity

## Power Systems & Magic

### Resonance System

Resonance is a mysterious and uncanny force manifesting as luck, blessings, curses, and strange inventions. It functions in three tiers: Common (minor effects), Advanced (significant influence), Ultimate (life-changing events). Resonances include Mystical (luck/misfortune), Divine (blessings/miracles), Necromantic (curses/spirits), and Technological (inventions/gear).

**Casting Method:** Unpredictable manifestation - cannot be directly controlled  
**Mana Source:** Enigmatic frontier forces, possibly tied to ancient lands or Gold Rush awakening  
**Skill Requirements:** Resonance unlocks cost points (2/4/8 for tiers), statistics d4-d8 typical

### Secondary Systems

No direct magic control - Resonance effects are enigmatic and tier-based, not spell-casting.

## Signature Quests

### Repeatable Activities

- **Gold Hunt:** Assist prospectors in finding veins through labor, protection, or Resonance enhancement
- **Posse Duty:** Join lawmen in chasing outlaws or defending towns
- **Trail Ambush Defense:** Combat the recurring "Troublemaker" threat

### Major Quest Lines

**"The Big Claim"**
Race to claim and protect a major gold vein, involving negotiation, combat, and outsmarting rivals.

- *Rewards:* Wealth, faction alliances, Resonance insights

**"The Great Train Robbery"**  
Protect or rob a valuable rail shipment, choosing sides in frontier conflicts.

- *Stakes:* Loss of shipment could cripple economy or fuel outlaw power

**"Resonance Mystery"**
Unravel strange occurrences tied to Resonance enigmas.

- *Rewards:* Understanding of Resonance origins, powerful abilities
