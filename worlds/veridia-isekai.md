# ISEKAI WORLD: Veridia, The Glitched World

## World Classification

**World** **Type:** C-Rank - Game-Like World / Mundane Earth 2.0 Hybrid

**Danger** **Level:** High. Urban decay meets high-fantasy lethality. Survival is not guaranteed; it's a commodity.

**Power** **Ceiling:** SSS-Rank. The threats range from back-alley thugs with enchanted pipes (F-Tier) to reality-warping Glitch-Gods that exist in the System's corrupted source code (SSS-Tier).

## World Overview

One day, the world didn't end. It just... *glitched*. They call it the Fracture. The sky flickered, displaying code for a moment, and then the Bleeds appeared: shimmering violet portals to other realities, teeming with impossible creatures and forgotten physics. With the Bleeds came the System. Everyone, overnight, had a Status screen in their head. Life became a game, and the only way to level up was to risk your life inside those portals, now called "Dungeons."

Society fractured. Megacorporations rose to control the most stable Bleeds, turning them into privatized resource mines for Etherium, the raw mana-stuff that powers this new world. Below them, a chaotic ecosystem of Hunter Guilds formed—everything from corporate-sponsored armies to desperate slum-gangs—all fighting for access to the scraps. They guard the public Dungeons with brutal efficiency, not to keep people safe, but to control the flow of loot and camp the respawn points of their rivals.

This is Veridia. A neon-drenched, rain-slicked metropolis built on the ruins of the old world. A place where cybernetic augmentation clashes with runic magic, where your reputation with a faction is worth more than your life, and where the spiciest commodity isn't a drug, but a rumor of a Dungeon with an exploitable loot table. Welcome to the grind.

**Climate/Environment:** Perpetual twilight drizzle in the megacities, illuminated by holographic ads. The areas around Bleeds are often warped, featuring impossible geography or weather patterns leaking from the Dungeon within.

## Starting Scenarios

### A) The Classic - "The Gutter Rat"

You awaken in a filthy back-alley of the Undercity, your old life a fading dream. You have no gear, no currency, and no guild affiliation. The System is a confusing mess in your head, and the only thing you own is the tattered jumpsuit on your back.

**Starting** **Kit:** Tattered jumpsuit, a rusty pipe (F-Tier).

**Initial** **Challenges:** Hunger, avoiding predatory low-level gangs, finding a way to enter a Dungeon without being killed by the Guilds guarding it.

**First** **Contact:** A desperate F-Rank Hunter offering to split a dangerous, unsanctioned Dungeon run.

### B) The Academy - "The Corpo-Cadet"

Your soul was slotted into the body of a new recruit at the prestigious Genesis Dynamics Hunter Academy. You live in a sterile dorm, your every move monitored. Here, you will be molded into a loyal corporate asset, a cog in the machine that bleeds reality dry for profit.

**Starting** **Kit:** Genesis Dynamics uniform, training weapon (D-Tier), a 50-credit weekly stipend.

**Initial** **State:** Neutral with Genesis Dynamics. Your first test is a simulated Dungeon run.

**Academy** **Features:** State-of-the-art training facilities, mandatory corporate ethics classes, a strict demerit system.

**Social** **Dynamics:** Ruthless rivals vying for top rankings, an enigmatic senior mentor, and the ever-present threat of being "decommissioned" for failure.

### C) The Guild - "The FNG"

You come to in a grimy guild hall, the smell of stale synth-booze in the air. A half-erased tattoo on your arm marks you as the newest member of the "Rust Dogs" Guild. You've just signed a contract that gives the Guild 70% of all your earnings. Welcome to the bottom of the food chain, kid.

**Starting** **Kit:** Mismatched leather armor (D-Tier), a chipped combat knife (D-Tier), a 20-credit debt to the guild bar.

**Guild** **Options:** You're stuck with the Rust Dogs for now.

**Initial** **Reputation:** Neutral with your own guild, Hostile with their rivals, the "Scrap Vultures."

**First** **Missions:** "Clear the Glitched Rats from the sewer entrance," or "Act as a distraction while the senior members hit a corporate convoy."

### D) The Party - "The Replacement"

You awaken to the roar of a monster and a frantic scream of "Get up, newbie!" You're in the middle of a Dungeon, your memories fabricated by the System. You joined this party a week ago. The person whose spot you're filling? Their blood is still drying on the wall.

**Starting** **Kit:** C-Tier gear appropriate to your role (Tank, Striker, or Techie).

**Party** **Composition:** A grizzled, cynical leader; a jumpy tech-mage who handles the hacking; and a stoic, cybered-up brute.

**Initial** **Mission:** Survive the rest of this C-Rank Dungeon and kill the "Protocol Hound" boss that ate your predecessor.

### E) The Deep End - "The Repo'd"

You gasp awake on a cold metal slab in a back-alley cybernetics clinic. A fresh, painful scar covers half your torso, and a cheap, glitchy cyber-arm is grafted to your shoulder. You were organ-jacked, but the syndicate that took your parts left you with a "gift"... and an insurmountable debt. You work for them now.

**Starting** **Kit:** Hospital gown, one glitchy cybernetic arm (D-Tier, prone to malfunction), a 10,000-credit debt to the Crimson Chain syndicate.

**Immediate** **Threats:** Your new arm is attracting unwanted attention, and your first debt-payment is due in 24 hours.

**Survival** **Rewards:** Clearing your debt could let you keep the chrome. Impressing the syndicate might unlock the [Syndicate Enforcer] hidden class.

### F) The Transplant - "The Silver Spoon"

Your soul was the premium option. You awaken in a penthouse suite, your new body belonging to the heir of a major corporate family. You have the best gear, the best training, and a guaranteed spot in the elite Vanguard Guild. Everyone expects greatness, and your rivals are just waiting for you to slip up so they can take everything.

**Starting** **Kit:** Full set of B-Tier custom gear, 1,000 credits, a personal AI assistant.

**Recruiter:** Your new "father," a ruthless executive at Mitsuhama-Aethel Corp.

**Purpose:** To become the #1 ranked Hunter and a living PR mascot for the corporation, no matter the cost.

### G) The Regression - "The Server Reset"

You were one of the best. You reached the S-Tier, fought in the deepest Bleeds. And you died, watching the SSS-Tier Glitch-God, "The Administrator," begin the final data-wipe of reality. Now, you're back in your old, un-augmented body on the morning of the Fracture. You have a few hours before it all starts again. This time, you'll be ready.

**Regression** **Path** **(Awakening):** You are Level 1, but retain all knowledge of future events, hidden dungeons, and game-breaking exploits. Your true power is locked, slowly unsealing as you level up.

**Starting** **Kit:** Whatever you had on you that morning.

**Impending** **Disaster:** The Fracture event. Your first goal is to get your hands on a Unique-grade item you know will spawn in the chaos.

## Geography & Major Locations

### The Sprawl

* **Aethelburg** **Central:** The gleaming corporate heart of the city. Towers of chrome and glass. Home to Genesis Dynamics and The Vanguard.
* **The** **Undercity:** A chaotic, multi-layered slum built beneath Central. The territory of the Rust Dogs, the Crimson Chain, and countless other low-tier factions.
* **The** **Bleed** **Zone** **(BZ-07):** A cordoned-off quarantine zone where a massive, unstable Bleed has permanently warped the landscape into a crystalline forest patrolled by Code Wraiths.

## Factions & Politics

### Major Factions

**Mitsuhama-Aethel** **Corp** **(Lawful** **Evil)**

* *Philosophy:* Reality is a resource to be monetized. Control the Bleeds, control the future.
* *Methods:* Corporate espionage, hostile takeovers of guilds, political assassination, PR campaigns.
* *Leadership:* The cold and calculating CEO Masamune Ito.

**The** **Hunter's** **Guild** **Consortium** **(True** **Neutral)**

* *Philosophy:* Maintain order among Hunters, for a price. We don't care what you do, as long as you pay your dues.
* *Methods:* Issuing official licenses, running the Hunter leaderboards, mediating disputes (and taking a cut).

**The** **Unlinked** **(Chaotic** **Good)**

* *Philosophy:* The System is a chain, and the corporations are the masters. We will be free.
* *Methods:* Guerilla warfare, hacking corporate servers, liberating enslaved populaces within Dungeons.
* *Leadership:* The anonymous entity known only as "Gl1tch."

## Power Systems & Magic

### System-Assisted Channeling

The System acts as an OS for reality. Magic isn't about wisdom, it's about processing power. Spells are "software" that can be bought, looted, or hacked. Casting is fast, efficient, and requires Mana Points (MP).

**Casting** **Method:** System-Assisted. Think the spell's name, pay the MP cost.

**Mana** **Source:** Personal energy pool, replenished by consumables or special Etherium batteries.

**Skill** **Requirements:** A high INT stat allows you to run more complex "spell-programs."

### Secondary Systems

* **Techno-Mancy:** The art of fusing Etherium-laced hardware with the System. This creates cybernetics that boost stats, smart-rifles that cast spells, and drones that can hack enemy software.

## Isekai Integration Elements

### Modern Knowledge Applications

* **Programming:** A skilled programmer can learn to directly interface with the System, essentially becoming a reality-hacker. This is the path to the hidden [System Whisperer] class.
* **Engineering:** Knowledge of physics and mechanics allows for the creation of magitech weapons like railguns and power armor far superior to local designs.
* **Economics:** The Hunter economy is a volatile stock market. Someone with Earth knowledge can predict trends, corner the market on rare crafting materials, and bankrupt rivals.

### Cheat Skill Synergies

* **[Great** **Sage]:** In a world run by a literal computer system, this talent effectively makes you a co-administrator with limitless analytical power.
* **[Skill** **Steal]:** Imagine stealing not just a sword skill, but a corporation's proprietary software for a new cybernetic.

### Romance & Relationship Opportunities

Relationships in Veridia are a dangerous game of trust. Is that brilliant techie helping you because she likes you, or is she a corporate spy? Is your stoic bodyguard loyal to you, or the paycheck? Romance is often a luxury, a weakness to be exploited, or a desperate alliance against a world that wants you dead. True, selfless affection is the rarest, most S-Tier loot of all.
