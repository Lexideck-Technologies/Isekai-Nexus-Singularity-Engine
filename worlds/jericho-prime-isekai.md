# ISEKAI WORLD: JERICHO-PRIME

## World Classification

**World Type:** [Rank AA] - Mecha Weird Wild West / Dystopian Wasteland / Lost Tech Hybrid
**Danger Level:** Extreme (Environmental hazards, Tech-Disparity, Supernatural Phenomena)
**Power Ceiling:** A-Rank (Lost-Tech "Colossi" & Eldritch Mutations)

## World Overview

The Galactic Federation collapsed three centuries ago in a fiery cataclysm known as "The Great Silence." Jericho-Prime, a dusty rim-world, was cut off from the supply lines. Civilization regressed rapidly. Today, 99% of the population lives in a 19th-century frontier existence—wooden towns, saloons, horses, and six-shooters. But the shadow of the past looms large.

The wealthy elite, known as the **"Iron Barons,"** hoard the remaining functional technology. They rule from fortress-cities powered by dying fusion reactors, guarding their greatest treasures: **The Colossi**. These are ancient, battle-scarred mecha from the Federation era. In a world of revolvers, a single functional Colossus is a god of war. The atmosphere is thick with "Ghost-Static"—lingering psychic echoes of the billions who died in the Silence, causing machines to develop personalities and the dead to rest uneasily.

**Climate/Environment:** A scorched, arid desert planet. The days are blindingly hot; the nights are freezing and haunted by "Whisper-Winds." The only water comes from deep-bore wells controlled by the Barons.

## Starting Scenarios

### A) The Classic - "The Drifter"

[Lone survivor scenario - awakening in a shallow grave outside a ghost town]

**Starting Kit:** Dusty poncho, a rusty projectile revolver (F-Tier), a canteen of stale water, and a strange, pulsating "Key-Chip" around your neck.
**Initial Challenges:** Dehydration, coyotes the size of wolves, and amnesia.
**First Contact:** An old, blind prospector who claims he can "hear the metal singing" in your pocket.

### B) The Academy - "The Gear-Squire"

[Indentured servant scenario - The Citadel of Baron Kross]

**Starting Kit:** Grease-stained overalls, basic toolkit, access to the Baron's Hangar (Restricted).
**Initial State:** "Indentured Servant" (Serf). You are one of the few with the aptitude to clean the Colossi.
**Academy Features:** There are no classes, only brutal apprenticeships under paranoid "Tech-Priests" who hoard manuals like holy scripture.
**Social Dynamics:** Backstabbing among apprentices vying for the Baron's favor to become a pilot (a "Rider").

### C) The Guild - "The Pony Express"

[Professional organization scenario - The Courier's Guild]

**Starting Kit:** A cybernetically enhanced horse (C-Tier Mount), a repeater rifle, leather duster.
**Guild Options:**

* *The Couriers:* Delivering mail and small tech components between settlements.
* *The Regulators:* Guns for hire who hunt bounties.
**Initial Reputation:** Neutral.
**First Missions:** Deliver a replacement fusion-cell to a distant outpost before the lights go out and the "Hollow Men" attack.

### D) The Party - "The Train Robbers"

[Pre-existing group scenario - A gang looking to score big]

**Starting Kit:** Dynamite, sawed-off shotgun, a fast horse.
**Party Composition:**

* *The Face:* A charming card shark with a hidden laser-derringer.
* *The Muscle:* A mute brute wielding a sledgehammer made from a mech piston.
* *The Witch:* A tribal shaman who can calm machine spirits.
**Initial Mission:** Intercept a heavy-armor steam train carrying a shipment of off-world "Blue-Gold" (Fuel).

### E) The Deep End - "The Hanging Tree"

[High-risk scenario - About to be executed for a crime you didn't commit]

**Starting Kit:** A noose around your neck, hands tied.
**Immediate Threats:** The trapdoor is about to open. A raid by "Scrap-Skins" (cyborg cannibals) interrupts the execution.
**Survival Rewards:** If you escape the chaos, you find a crashed, critical-condition Colossus pilot who grants you his machine before dying.

### F) The Transplant - "The Off-Worlder"

[High-powered start - Arriving on one of the rare Star-Drops]

**Starting Kit:** High-tech distinct environment suit, a fully charged energy pistol (A-Tier), universal translator.
**Recruiter:** The **Star-Captain Valeros** (One of the few traders with a functioning starship).
**Purpose:** Locate a specific "Pre-Silence" facility buried under the sands that contains the codes to reactivate the planetary defense grid.

### G) The Regression - "The Old Sheriff"

[A legendary Lawman who died defending the town, waking up 20 years in the past]

**Regression Path:** Time Travel (Mental).
**Starting Kit:** Your old badge, a pristine "Peacemaker" Revolver (B-Tier), and knowledge of where every bandit hideout is.
**Impending Disaster:** The "Red-Sand Massacre"—where the Crimson Baron wipes out the entire valley.

## Geography & Major Locations

### The High Desert (The Frontier)

* **Purgatory Creek:** A wooden shantytown built around a massive, half-buried Colossus arm. The fingers serve as support beams for the saloon.
* **The Rust-Canyons:** A labyrinth of jagged red rock where wind blowing through old metal debris sounds like screaming.

### The Gilded Zones (Elite Territory)

* **Oasis Prime:** A walled paradise with running water, electric lights, and green grass. Ruled by the Governor.
* **The Iron Spire:** A fortress built inside the carcass of a crashed Federation Dreadnought.

### The Dead Lands

* **The Silicon Graveyard:** Miles of destroyed mechs from the final battle of the Silence. Rumored to be haunted by "Machine Ghosts."

## Factions & Politics

### Major Factions

**The Iron Barons (Lawful Evil):**

* *Philosophy:* Might makes right. Technology belongs to the worthy.
* *Methods:* Feudal oppression. They tax the towns for food and offer "protection" with their Colossi.
* *Leadership:* A council of five warlords, led by **Governor Sterling**.
* *Reputation Effects:* Fear/Hatred from commoners, Respect from elites.

**The Pale Riders (Chaotic Neutral):**

* *Philosophy:* The machines are cursed. We must return to the old ways.
* *Methods:* Sabotage, assassinating technicians, using "Hex-Magic" (Psychic disruptions).
* *Leadership:* **Mother Mercy** (A blind seer).
* *Reputation Effects:* Viewed as terrorists by Barons, saviors by the superstitious.

**The Off-World Trade Authority (True Neutral):**

* *Philosophy:* Profit from the scraps.
* *Methods:* They land once a year to trade rare fuel/parts for raw minerals. They do not interfere in local politics.

## Power Systems & Magic

### The Echo & Old-Tech

**Primary System:** **"The Echo" (Psionics/Resonance).**
The cataclysm that destroyed the Federation left a psychic scar. Sensitive individuals can "hear" electronics.

* **Mechanic:** *Whispering.* You can sense active mechs through walls.
* **Combat:** *Dead-Eye.* Focus your Echo to slow down perception of time (Bullet Time).

**Secondary System:** **Lost-Tech.**
There is no manufacturing. Every laser rifle, every fusion cell, every mech servo is a relic. They cannot be made, only repaired.

* **Economy:** A functioning AA battery is worth a gold bar. A Mech Fusion Core is worth a kingdom.

### Arch-Villains (The Lords of Rust)

**Governor Silas "Golden-Eye" Sterling - (AA-Tier Baron):**

* *Location:* Oasis Prime.
* *Power:* Pilots **"The Sovereign"**, a pristine, golden-plated Command Mech with a working energy shield (unheard of).
* *Motivation:* Maintain the status quo. He hoards the off-world supplies to keep the population desperate and obedient.
* *Weakness:* His mech consumes fuel at an alarming rate; he can't fight prolonged battles.

**"The Coroner" - (A-Tier Mad Scientist):**

* *Location:* A mobile laboratory-train that travels the rails.
* *Power:* Controls a swarm of spider-like cyborgs. He kidnaps townsfolk to "upgrade" them into compliant workers.
* *Motivation:* Believes flesh is weak and seeks to merge all humanity with machines to survive the harsh planet.
* *Weakness:* His physical body is frail and kept alive by a massive tank on his back.

**El Diablo Meca - (S-Tier Myth):**

* *Location:* Unknown.
* *Power:* A sentient AI mech that has rebuilt itself with jagged scrap metal and bones. It creates localized dust storms.
* *Motivation:* Destruction of all order. It is the ghost of the war given form.
* *Weakness:* The original "Kill Code" buried in the Federation archives.

## Isekai Integration Elements

### Modern Knowledge Applications

* **Electricity:** Building a simple wind turbine or water wheel generator to create electric light is seen as "Sorcery" or "Lost Tech" and instantly makes you a VIP.
* **Chemistry:** Making smokeless powder (gunpowder) or basic antibiotics.
* **Logistics:** The Barons are terrible at supply chains. Modern logistics can starve a Baron out of his fortress without firing a shot.

### Cheat Skill Synergies

* **[Repair/Mending]:** In a world where parts are finite, the ability to magically fix a cracked fusion seal or un-jam a rusted 300-year-old railgun is the ultimate cheat.
* **[Talk to Machines]:** Literally conversing with the "Machine Spirits" (AIs) in the Colossi allows the player to pilot mechs that reject everyone else.

### Romance & Relationship Opportunities

* **The Baron's Child:** A Romeo/Juliet dynamic with the rebellious heir to a tyrant.
* **The Saloon Singer:** A spy for the Pale Riders who uses her position to gather intel.

## Signature Quests

### Repeatable Activities

* **Caravan Guard:** Protecting water wagons from Dust-Sharks.
* **Scavenging:** Delving into the "Silence Zones" (Radioactive ruins) to find working circuit boards.
* **High Noon Duels:** One-on-one pistol duels in the street for honor and credits.

### Major Quest Lines

**"The Train Heist of the Century"**
The Governor is transporting a **Terraforming Core** (worth billions) to his private vault. You have to assemble a crew, stop a train moving at 200mph, fight off his elite mech guards, and steal it to save the dying planet.

* *Rewards:* The Core (Can restore a region to lush greenery), Billions in credits, AA-Tier Reputation.

**"The Ghost Signal"**
You find a beacon in a dead pilot's hand. It points to a Federation Bunker that was sealed before the Silence. Inside is a pristine hangar of untouched mechs.

* *Stakes:* If the Barons find it, they will enslave the world forever. You must get there first.

## Unique Jericho-Prime Features

### The Fuel Economy

* **Feature:** Mechs are powerful but "thirsty." Moving a Colossus costs **Blue-Gold**. Combat is often about positioning and intimidation rather than spraying fire, because running out of fuel mid-fight means death.

### The Duel Protocol

* **Cultural Element:** Even Barons respect the "Duel." Disputes are often settled by two pilots standing 100 paces apart and firing a single shot.

## Achievement System

### Frontier Justice

* **"Quick Draw":** Win a duel in under 0.5 seconds.
* **"Horse Whisperer":** Tame a wild cyborg-stallion.

### Tech Mastery

* **"Resurrection":** Bring a "dead" Colossus back to life using only scrap parts.

### Ultimate Achievements

* **"Sheriff of the Stars":** Unite the towns, overthrow the Barons, and establish a new planetary law.
* **"The Rainmaker":** Activate the Terraforming Core and bring rain to Jericho-Prime for the first time in 300 years.
