# ISEKAI WORLD: VESPER-ENGINE

## World Classification

**World Type:** [Rank A] - Silver City Shadows (Noir Urban Fantasy / Teslapunk Mecha / Global Ecumenopolis Hybrid)
**Danger Level:** Very High (Global infrastructure collapse, Nocturnal Apex Predators)
**Power Ceiling:** S-Rank (Soul-Bound Colossi & The Empress's Sorcery)

## World Overview

Vesper-Engine is a planet-sized city (Ecumenopolis) locked in a perpetual Victorian era of brass, steam, and soot. There are no forests, no oceans—only layers of rusted iron districts, endless factories, and towering gothic spires that pierce the smog. The planet is dying; the "Great Gears" that regulate the climate and gravity are grinding to a halt.

But the true horror begins at sunset. In this world, the dead do not always stay dead. Through a process known as **"The Rebinding,"** wandering souls are pulled into empty mechanical shells—**Reliquaries** (Mecha). These are not mindless zombies; they are towering, steam-driven suits of armor possessed by human spirits. They are constructed in secret by the enigmatic "Ossuary Guild" and hidden in the deepest, darkest crevices of the city—abandoned subway silos, flooded basements, and sealed cathedral domes.

When the sun sets, the Reliquaries awaken. Their cores glow with "Ecto-Volts." They flood the streets. Some retain their humanity and patrol as guardians. Others are driven mad by the trauma of death and raid the living for "Vitality." The living lock their iron shutters and pray the gaslights don't go out.

**Climate/Environment:** Perpetual smog. It rains oil and ash. The days are dim and yellow; the nights are pitch black, illuminated only by flickering gaslamps and the glowing eyes of the Reliquaries.

## Starting Scenarios

### A) The Classic - "The Cold Awakening"

[Soul-transfer scenario - You awaken *as* a Reliquary]

**Starting Kit:** You possess a rusty, D-Tier "Stoker-Class" chassis (15 feet tall, steam-powered), an integrated coal furnace, and a failing memory.
**Initial Challenges:** The sun is rising. Sunlight burns Ectoplasm. You must find a dark place to hide before you are "exorcised" by the solar rays.
**First Contact:** A terrified street urchin who saw you move and is debating whether to scavenge your parts or ask for help.

### B) The Academy - "The Aether-University"

[Educational institution scenario - The Royal Institute of Technomancy]

**Starting Kit:** Scholar's robes, a basic "Spirit-Wrench," and a scholarship to the Department of Necro-Kinetics.
**Initial State:** Neutral. You are a "New Blood" student.
**Academy Features:** Dissection labs where machines are autopsied like bodies, séances to contact machine spirits.
**Social Dynamics:** The elitist nobles who see Technomancy as their birthright vs. the "Grease-Monkeys" (Commoners).

### C) The Guild - "The Lamp-Lighters"

[Professional organization scenario - The City Guard/Maintenance]

**Starting Kit:** Reinforced leather coat, a "Sun-Stick" (Magnesium flare spear), and a map of the District 9 pipelines.
**Guild Options:**

* *The Lamp-Lighters:* Maintain the streetlights that repel weaker spirits.
* *The Pipe-Runners:* Navigate the dangerous under-city to fix steam leaks.
**Initial Reputation:** F-Rank "Wick-Trimmer."
**First Missions:** Escort a food convoy through a "Blackout Zone" where the gaslights have failed and the Rogue Reliquaries are hunting.

### D) The Party - "The Grave Robbers"

[Pre-existing group scenario - Illegal scavengers]

**Starting Kit:** Crowbars, spirit-goggles, a steam-wagon.
**Party Composition:**

* *The Face:* A disgraced noble selling secrets.
* *The Muscle:* A half-construct human with a steam-arm.
* *The Medium:* Can hear where the "good loot" (high-quality dormant mechs) is hidden.
**Initial Mission:** Break into a sealed factory in the Industrial Sector to steal a "Noble-Class" Chassis before it wakes up tonight.

### E) The Deep End - "The Twilight Zone"

[High-risk scenario - Stranded on the surface at dusk]

**Starting Kit:** Normal clothes, a heavy wrench.
**Immediate Threats:** The sun is setting. The sirens are wailing. The ground is shaking as thousands of Reliquaries claw their way out of the sewers around you.
**Survival Rewards:** If you survive the "Night of Long Knives," you gain the **[Night-Blooded]** trait (Stealth bonus in darkness).

### F) The Transplant - "The Royal Artificer"

[High-powered start - Summoned by Her Majesty]

**Starting Kit:** Master-work tools, a personal laboratory in the Spire, unrestrained access to the Royal Archives.
**Recruiter:** **Empress Vespera IV.**
**Purpose:** The "Heart of the City"—the planetary main engine—is failing. You have been brought here to fix it before the planet freezes.

### G) The Regression - "The Clockwork Messiah"

[A revolutionary leader who failed to overthrow the Empress, sent back]

**Regression Path:** Time Travel (Mental).
**Starting Kit:** Knowledge of the "Kill-Codes" for the Royal Guard Reliquaries, a hidden stash of A-Tier weapons.
**Impending Disaster:** The "Great Purge," where the Empress unleashes the "Legion of Silencers" to wipe out the poor districts.

## Geography & Major Locations

### The Crown Spire (The Upper City)

* **The Gilded Cage:** The highest layer of the city, above the smog. Here, the aristocracy lives in glass palaces, protected by "Paladins" (Golden Reliquaries).
* **The Solarium:** A massive array of mirrors that traps sunlight to power the upper city, leaving the bottom in darkness.

### The Soot-Wards (The Middle City)

* **District 12 (The Foundry):** An endless maze of smokestacks and factories. The noise is deafening. This is where the majority of humanity lives and works.
* **The Brass Market:** A bazaar where you can buy anything from coal to bottled souls.

### The Undercity (The Lower Depths)

* **The Rust-Ocean:** The lowest levels are flooded with toxic sludge and oil. Here, ancient, feral Reliquaries from centuries ago roam like deep-sea monsters.

## Factions & Politics

### Major Factions

**The Iron Crown (Lawful Evil):**

* *Philosophy:* Order requires sacrifice. The City must survive, even if the people don't.
* *Methods:* Secret police, public executions, strict rationing of Techs.
* *Leadership:* **Empress Vespera IV**.
* *Reputation Effects:* Fear from commoners, obedience from the military.

**The Ossuary Guild (True Neutral/Secretive):**

* *Philosophy:* The flesh is temporary; the machine is eternal. We provide the vessels.
* *Methods:* They are the only ones who know how to build Reliquaries. They sell to the highest bidder but answer to no one.
* *Leadership:* **"The Tinsmith"** (Identity Unknown).
* *Reputation Effects:* Mysticism. To cross them is to ensure your soul finds no housing after death.

**The Gear-Breakers (Chaotic Good):**

* *Philosophy:* The machines are abominations. Let the dead sleep. Smash the engines.
* *Methods:* Bombings, sabotage of Reliquary factories, exorcisms.
* *Leadership:* **Father John Cog** (An ex-priest).

## Power Systems & Magic

### Ecto-Steam & Technomancy

**Primary System:** **Spirit-Binding (Necromancy).**
Magic is the manipulation of the soul.

* *The Source:* When people die, their "Spark" lingers in the smog.
* *The Tech:* **"Spirit-Valves"** compress this energy to animate cold iron.
* *Skill:* **[Necro-Kinetics]**. Used to pilot Reliquaries, repair them, or "Command" them.

**Secondary System:** **Technomancy.**
The physical repair of the world. Because the world is "conceptually" broken, fixing a gear isn't just mechanics; it requires willpower. A Master Technomancer can hit a broken generator with a wrench and "wills" it to work.

**Life Magic:**
Extremely rare. Used by the "Bio-Mancers" to heal the living, who suffer from "Soot-Lung" and malnutrition.

### Arch-Villains (The Architects of Decay)

**Empress Vespera IV - "The Iron Widow" (AA-Tier Technomancer):**

* *Location:* The Throne Room of the Crown Spire.
* *Power:* She is physically frail but connected via cables to the **City Core**. She can control the gravity, steam pressure, and doors of the entire planet.
* *Motivation:* She believes she is the only thing keeping the planet from falling apart. She seeks to turn *everyone* into machines to achieve "Perfect Order."
* *Weakness:* Disconnecting her from the Core would leave her powerless (and the city chaotic).

**Archimedes "The Tinsmith" - (S-Tier Builder):**

* *Location:* Unknown (Rumored to be in a pocket dimension inside a pocketwatch).
* *Power:* He builds the S-Tier Reliquaries. He can create life from metal. He is the source of the global chaos.
* *Motivation:* He is an artist. He finds the chaos of the night "beautiful." He builds specific Mechas to cause specific tragedies, treating the world as his opera.
* *Weakness:* His obsession with "Dramatic Irony."

## Isekai Integration Elements

### Modern Knowledge Applications

* **Electrical Engineering:** The world runs on crude steam and "Ecto-Volts." Introducing AC/DC current, capacitors, or transistors would revolutionize the power grid and make the player the wealthiest person on the planet.
* **Civil Engineering:** Understanding structural integrity allows the player to spot which skyscrapers are about to collapse before they do.
* **Psychology:** Reliquaries are driven by trauma. Modern therapy techniques could "cure" a Hollow-Shell, turning a monster back into a rational ally.

### Cheat Skill Synergies

* **[Light Magic]:** In a world of darkness and undead constructs, even a basic [Flash] spell is a weapon of mass destruction.
* **[Item Box]:** Smuggling heavy mech parts through the heavily guarded checkpoints is impossible for locals, but easy for the player.

### Romance & Relationship Opportunities

* **The Ghost in the Machine:** A romance with a Reliquary pilot. Can you find a way to make them human again, or do you accept their mechanical form?
* **The Tired Empress:** A political romance where you try to melt the icy heart of the despot who is crushed by the weight of a dying world.

## Signature Quests

### Repeatable Activities

* **Exorcism Patrols:** Clearing a district of Hollow-Shells before the workers' shift begins.
* **Soul-Courier:** Transporting a trapped soul to their family's purchased Reliquary without it escaping.

### Major Quest Lines

**"The Midnight Sun"**
The Empress plans to blot out the sun permanently using a "Smog-Shield" so her armies can march 24/7. You must sabotage the atmospheric generators.

* *Stakes:* Eternal darkness, but total tyranny.

**"The Maker's Mark"**
You find a blueprint signed by "The Tinsmith" hidden inside a rogue mech. It details a "God-Machine"—a Reliquary big enough to house the soul of the planet itself.

* *Rewards:* The ability to pilot the Planet (or destroy it).

## Unique Vesper-Engine Features

### The Sunlight Mechanic

* **Constraint:** Reliquaries cannot function in direct sunlight. If the player pilots one, they *must* find cover by dawn. Being caught out drains HP rapidly and causes "Soul-Burn" (Permanent stat loss). This turns gameplay into a tense race against the clock.

### The Repair Deficit

* **Economy:** New parts do not exist. Everything must be scavenged or refurbished. Healing potions don't work on Mechs; you need a welder and spare plating.

## Achievement System

### Mechanic's Pride

* **"Percussive Maintenance":** Fix a critical engine failure by hitting it with a wrench (Requires high LCK).
* **"Ghost in the Shell":** Successfully transfer a loved one's soul into a Reliquary.

### Ultimate Achievements

* **"Sunrise":** Fix the Planetary Atmospheric Scrubbers and clear the sky for the first time in 1,000 years.
* **"Deus Ex Machina":** Defeat the Tinsmith and take control of the Ossuary Guild.
