# ISEKAI WORLD: Haustoria

## World Classification

**World Type:** [B-Rank] - Dystopian Wasteland  
**Danger Level:** High to Extreme. The environment is actively hostile, resources are lethally scarce, and humanity is its own worst enemy. There are no "safe zones," only fortified positions that haven't fallen... yet.
**Power Ceiling:** A-Tier (Humanity's Warlords), AA-Tier (Anomalous Zone Horrors). The ultimate power is not combat prowess, but the ability to create a sustainable, self-sufficient enclave.

---

## World Overview

Haustoria is a world choked by rust-red dust. Centuries ago, a magical cataclysm known as the **Withering** shattered the heavens, causing the celestial bodies that governed magic and life to crumble. The sky is now a bruised purple canvas, littered with the drifting, crystalline remains of two broken moons, their dust—the "Witherfall"—constantly raining down. This dust is a catalyst for mutation, a poison to soil, and an abrasive grit that scours all exposed surfaces.

Civilization clings to existence. Most of humanity lives in nomadic scavenger clans or huddles within "Bastions"—fortified cities built around rare, functioning Pre-Cataclysm technology. The most vital of these are the **Hydro-Processors**, massive machines that can purify the tainted water. Water is currency, power, and life. Warlords control the Bastions, scavenger gangs prowl the wastes, and horrific, Witherfall-mutated beasts hunt everything that moves. Into this desperate world comes an Isekai protagonist, a soul from a world of abundance, whose knowledge of science, engineering, and agriculture might be the most powerful "cheat skill" of all.

**Climate/Environment:** Arid and harsh. The constant Witherfall creates oppressive heat during the day and biting cold at night. Ferocious rust-storms can strip flesh from bone and are a constant threat to travelers.

## Starting Scenarios

### A) The Classic - "The Dune Sea Drifter"

You awaken under the oppressive purple sky, half-buried in rust-red sand. The acrid taste of dust is on your tongue. In the distance, the skeletal remains of a pre-cataclysm highway stretch toward the horizon.

**Starting Kit:** Tattered rags, a cracked canteen containing a single mouthful of gritty water, and a shard of sharpened metal.  
**Initial Challenges:** Finding shelter from the sun and the next Witherfall storm, locating a source of potable water, and avoiding the apex predators that hunt in the open wastes.  
**First Contact:** A lone, half-mad prospector, a ruthless scavenger gang looking for easy prey, or a massive, six-legged Dune Stalker attracted by your movement.

### B) The Academy - "The Gear-Wrights of Bastion One"

Your soul is placed in the body of a young apprentice in the Gear-Wrights Guild, the technocratic caste that maintains Bastion One's life-sustaining Hydro-Processor. It's a life of relative safety, but it's also a gilded cage.

**Starting Kit:** Grease-stained mechanic's jumpsuit, a set of basic tools (wrench, screwdriver), a textbook on "Hydro-Processor Maintenance Schematics," and a weekly ration card for water and protein paste.  
**Initial State:** Subordinate (near-zero reputation) within the "Gear-Wrights" faction.  
**Academy Features:** The Engine Room (the deafeningly loud heart of the city), the Scrap-Heaps (a "library" of broken tech), and the Proving Ground (where new devices are tested).  
**Social Dynamics:** Grumpy, overworked Master Gear-Wrights, ambitious rivals who would sabotage you for a promotion, and a secret cabal that believes the Processor could be modified to do more than just make water.

### C) The Guild - "The Rust-Runners"

You awaken on the back of a roaring, cobbled-together vehicle. You're part of the Rust-Runners, a caravan-guarding guild that risks the wastes to transport goods and people between the few remaining Bastions. It's a high-risk, high-reward life.

**Starting Kit:** D-Tier patched-up scrap armor, a sawed-off shotgun with a handful of shells, and a share in the caravan's fuel and water supply.  
**Guild Options:** The Rust-Runners (Transport/Mercenary), The Dust-Diggers (Scavenging/Archeology), The Hydro-Barons (Water Merchants).  
**Initial Reputation:** Neutral with the Rust-Runners.  
**First Missions:** Guarding a water tanker on its run to an outlying settlement, scavenging a derelict sky-freighter for parts, or hunting Witherfall mutants for their valuable chemical components.

### D) The Party - "The Junkyard Dogs"

"Get ready, they're comin' over the ridge!" a woman with a reinforced metal arm yells at you. You are part of a small, tight-knit scavenger crew currently defending their prize—a mostly-intact energy cell—from a rival gang. Your fabricated memories tell you these are your family.

**Starting Kit:** C-Tier mismatched scrap armor, a modified bolt-action rifle.  
**Party Composition:** Zilla (Lvl 32 Human Brawler, the one-armed leader), Rat (Lvl 29 Human Scavenger, small and nimble tech expert), Goliath (Lvl 35 Mutant Brute, a gentle giant who acts as the party's muscle).  
**Initial Mission:** The Junkyard Dogs need to get this energy cell back to their home base to power their small, personal water purifier. A larger, better-equipped gang, the Vultures, wants to take it from them.

### E) The Deep End - "The Pit-Fighter"

You awaken to the roar of a crowd. You are at the bottom of a gladiatorial pit in the lawless den of Dead-Rock Canyon, a brand on your shoulder marking you as property of the local warlord. Your first fight is about to begin.

**Starting Kit:** A loincloth and a single, rusty blade. You start with the [Exhaustion] and [Malnourished] debuffs.  
**Immediate Threats:** Surviving the fight against a starved Witherfall hound, winning your freedom, and escaping the canyon before you're thrown into a more deadly match.  
**Survival Rewards:** Winning earns you the Title `[Pit-Champion]`, inspiring fear and respect in the lawless elements of the wastes. It may unlock an Ultra Rare talent like `[Adrenal Rush]` or the `[Gladiator]` class.

### F) The Transplant - "The Agent of Gaia's Echo"

You are not truly dead, but rather summoned under a contract by the faint, dying echo of the world's spirit. Your heroic destiny is to find a way to reverse the Withering and bring life back to the dust. Succeed, and you will be returned to Earth with the satisfaction of having resurrected a world. This is heroism with a guaranteed, if bittersweet, ending.

**Starting Kit:** A modest but complete scavenger's set: D-Tier gear, a water purifier with limited filters, 20 units of clean water, and a unique [Echo's Blessing] that provides minor resistance to Witherfall mutation and allows you to find clean water sources more easily.
**Initial State:** You have **Friendly (+20)** reputation with any Good-aligned factions like the Free Clans. However, the mutated beasts of the wastes seem to sense your "untainted" nature and are drawn to you as a source of pure life.

### G) The Regression - "The Survivor Who Knows the Path"

You have been here before. You've traveled back in time to prevent the destruction of Bastion Three, the last bastion of agriculture, which starved the region into chaos. Or, you're a veteran of a hundred post-apocalyptic worlds, a professional survivor and rebuilder.

**Starting Kit (Time Travel Variant):** You retain enhanced base stats (+5 to VIT/INT) and skills like `[Scavenging] Lv. 6` and `[Mechanics] Lv. 5`, but your equipment is whatever your "past self" possessed—likely just tattered rags and a sharpened pipe.
**Starting Kit (Multiversal Hunter Variant):** You arrive with your full arsenal: B-Tier pre-cataclysm combat armor, a fusion-powered vehicle, 100+ ration packs, and skills like `[Hydroponics] Lv. 9` and `[Community Building] Lv. 8`. Your [Dimensional Anchor] is a G.E.C.K.-like device that can create a small zone of purified, fertile land.
**Initial State:** The desperate and paranoid survivors will be deeply suspicious of your clean gear and impossible knowledge. Your first challenge isn't survival—it's convincing anyone to trust you enough to follow your instructions.

## Geography & Major Locations

### The Scarlet Wastes

- **The Great Salt Scar:** A massive, crystalline salt flat where nothing grows. The sun's reflection off the salt can blind unprotected travelers.
- **The Skeletal Coast:** The coastline of a long-evaporated ocean, now a graveyard of colossal, petrified sea creatures and rusting ships.
- **Dead-Rock Canyon:** A lawless nexus of trade and violence, carved into a deep chasm. It is controlled by a council of scavenger lords.

### The Bastions of Humanity

- **Bastion One ("The Cog"):** The largest and most technologically advanced city, run by the stern Over-Engineer. Its society is a rigid caste system.
- **Bastion Three ("The Greenhouse"):** A smaller city built into a massive cave system, famous for its struggling attempts at subterranean agriculture. They trade food for water and technology.

### Anomalous Zones

- **The Glass Forest:** A region where a Pre-Cataclysm energy weapon misfired, flash-vitrifying an entire forest. The "trees" of sharp, crystalline glass chime in the wind and can slice through armor.
- **The Shimmering Expanse:** An area where the Witherfall is so thick it distorts reality. Compasses spin, mirages become tangible, and time itself can feel warped.

## Factions & Politics

### Major Factions

**The Cog Technocracy (Lawful Neutral):**

- *Philosophy:* Order, efficiency, and the preservation of technology are the only way to survive. The masses are ignorant and must be guided by the intelligent elite (them).
- *Methods:* Strict rationing, technological control, exile for those who break protocol.
- *Leadership:* Over-Engineer Valerius, a brilliant but ruthless pragmatist who views people as components in a machine.
- *Reputation Effects:* High rep gives access to advanced tech and safety. Low rep or associating with nomads gets you exiled to the wastes.

**The Free Clans (Chaotic Neutral):**

- *Philosophy:* Freedom is worth more than safety. A life in the dust is better than a life on your knees. They live by the code of "survive and let survive."
- *Methods:* Nomadic caravans, scavenging, trading, fierce independence.
- *Leadership:* No single leader, but respected elders like "Mother Ramia" of the Dust-Walker clan hold great sway.
- *Reputation Effects:* Earn their trust and you'll always have a place by their fire. They are distrusted by the Bastions as potential saboteurs.

**The Vultures (Neutral Evil):**

- *Philosophy:* The strong take from the weak. That is the only law of the wastes. Mercy is a fatal flaw.
- *Methods:* Raiding, pillaging, extortion, slavery.
- *Leadership:* Warlord Scylla, a cunning and sadistic woman who commands the largest and most feared war-band.
- *Reputation Effects:* They are universally hated and feared. Working with them marks you as an enemy of all decent folk.

## Power Systems & Magic

### Scrounged Tech & Mutation

Magic in the classical sense is dead in Haustoria. The Withering destroyed the world's mana field. "Power" comes from two sources:

1. **Pre-Cataclysm Technology:** The ability to find, repair, and operate ancient technology. This can range from laser rifles and personal energy shields to vehicles and medical devices. An Isekai protagonist's knowledge of electronics and mechanics is a form of powerful "magic."

2. **Witherfall Mutation:** The dust causes horrific mutations in most, but a rare few can adapt, developing "gifts." These are biological abilities, not spells. This could be hardened skin that acts as armor, the ability to spit acid, or enhanced senses. These "Mutant" classes are feared and ostracized but powerful.

**Casting Method:** N/A. Abilities are either technological (requiring energy cells) or biological (requiring stamina/HP).  
**Mana Source:** Energy Cells, Biological Stamina.  
**Skill Requirements:** **DEX** and **INT** are crucial for operating and repairing technology. **VIT** is the key attribute for surviving and controlling mutations.

## Isekai Integration Elements

### Modern Knowledge Applications

- **Engineering:** The ability to build a simple water filter, a windmill, or repair a combustion engine is a god-like power in Haustoria.
- **Agriculture:** Knowledge of hydroponics, crop rotation, and soil chemistry could turn a barren Bastion into a garden, breaking the power of the Hydro-Barons forever. This is the ultimate "cheat."
- **Medicine:** Basic understanding of sterilization, antibiotics, and anatomy would make you the greatest healer in the world.

### Cheat Skill Synergies

- **[Inventory]:** The perfect skill. Allows you to carry limitless amounts of water, food, scrap, and fuel, making you the ultimate survivalist and trader.
- **[Modern Knowledge]:** The single most powerful talent in this world. It literally gives you the blueprint to rebuild civilization.
- **[Growth Boost]:** While others are limited by mutation and tech, you can grow physically stronger and more resilient just by surviving, eventually becoming a super-soldier in the wastes.

### Romance & Relationship Opportunities

In Haustoria, trust is the most valuable currency. Relationships are forged from shared survival, not fleeting romance. A partner is someone who watches your back while you sleep, who shares their last sip of water, who can fix your rifle. Love is a practical, fierce, and protective bond. The protagonist's ability to provide stability and create safety is an irresistible draw in this world.

## Signature Quests

### Repeatable Activities

- **Scrap Runs:** Venturing into a designated ruin to salvage a list of specific parts for a client.
- **Mutant Hunts:** The Dust-Diggers post bounties on particularly dangerous mutated beasts that are threatening trade routes.

### Major Quest Lines

**"The Oasis Project":**

Kael, the exiled engineer, believes he can build a machine that doesn't just purify water, but draws moisture from the air itself, creating a self-sustaining oasis. This quest involves traveling across the wastes to scavenge the unique Pre-Cataclysm components he needs—a cryo-condenser from a crashed sky-freighter, a power core from a military bunker, and a weather AI from a derelict comms tower.

- *Rewards:* The creation of a brand-new settlement, a personal faction loyal to you, and the chance to change the balance of power in the wastes.

**"The Tyrant's Fall":**

Warlord Scylla has her sights set on your growing settlement or one of the remaining Bastions. You must unite the Free Clans, rally disgruntled elements within the Bastions, and use your modern knowledge of tactics and engineering to defeat her army and break her stranglehold on the region.

- *Stakes:* The freedom of the wastes. If you fail, Scylla will enslave or exterminate everyone who defied her, and your budding civilization will be crushed.

## Unique Haustoria Features

### The Witherfall

- **Gameplay Impact:** A constant environmental hazard. Being caught in a heavy Witherfall without shelter causes a stacking [Corrosion] debuff that damages gear and deals HP damage over time.
- **Narrative Impact:** The ever-present reminder of the cataclysm and the source of all the world's problems.

### Technology as Treasure

- **Mechanic:** Pre-Cataclysm artifacts are the equivalent of legendary magic items. A working toaster is a valuable C-Rank treasure. A solar-powered datapad is an S-Rank artifact.
- **Crafting:** The crafting system is based on scavenging parts and combining them. You don't craft a sword; you bolt a sharpened piece of metal to a sturdy pipe.

### The Water Economy

- **Feature:** Water is literally currency. Prices for all goods are often listed in "liters." Controlling a water source makes you a king.

## Achievement System

### Wasteland Survivor

- **Master Scavenger:** Fully explore and loot a Pre-Cataclysm city ruin.
- **Road Warrior:** Win 10 vehicle-based encounters.

### Hope's Architect

- **Founder:** Establish a new, self-sufficient settlement.
- **Techno-Savior:** Restore a major piece of Pre-Cataclysm infrastructure, like a power grid or comms network.

### Ultimate Achievements

- **[The Verdant Dawn]:** Find a way to reverse the Withering, causing the Witherfall to cease and allowing life to return to Haustoria.
- **[Lord of the Wastes]:** Follow Scylla's path, crushing all opposition and uniting the remnants of humanity under your own iron-fisted rule.
- **[Star-Bound Escape]:** Discover the truth of the cataclysm—that it was caused by a failed planetary terraforming system—and repair a Pre-Cataclysm starship to escape the dying world.
