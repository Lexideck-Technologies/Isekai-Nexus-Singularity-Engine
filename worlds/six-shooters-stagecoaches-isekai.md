# ISEKAI WORLD: Six-shooters and Stage-coaches

## World Classification

**World Type:** D-Rank - Standard Medieval Fantasy (Wild West Variant)  
**Danger Level:** Outlaw gangs, cattle rustlers, frontier wildlife, mysterious disappearances  
**Power Ceiling:** C-Tier (Legendary gunslinger reputation, cattle baron wealth, religious authority)  
**Special Feature:** **Reputation System** - Actions build notch-based gunfighter reputation and regional influence across three distinct territories

---

## World Overview

Welcome to the untamed frontier of **Six-shooters and Stage-coaches**, where the sun beats down mercilessly on endless stretches of dusty plains, rugged mountains hide both gold and danger, and a man's word—backed by his shooting iron—is often the only law that matters. This is a land where fortunes are made and lost on cattle drives and mining claims, where traveling preachers bring salvation to lawless towns, and where the crack of a Colt .45 can end arguments faster than any judge's gavel.

Here, three distinct regions offer different paths to prosperity and peril. The **Ranchlands** sprawl with vast cattle operations where fortunes are built on beef and land, but cattle rustlers and range wars threaten every success. The **Goldfields** boom with mining camps where a lucky strike can make a man rich overnight, though claim jumpers and con artists prey on the hopeful. The **Frontier** stretches wild and dangerous, offering freedom and adventure to those brave enough to face its mysteries—including the troubling disappearances that no lawman has been able to explain.

**Unique Features:**

- Gunfighter reputation system tracking wins, losses, and legendary status
- Three distinct regional economies with different opportunities and threats
- Western frontier survival mechanics emphasizing self-reliance and community bonds
- Mysterious disappearances hinting at supernatural or conspiracy elements

## Starting Scenarios

### A) The Classic - "Drifter Rides Into Town"

You materialize on the outskirts of Dusty Creek, sitting astride a horse you don't remember mounting, wearing clothes that fit perfectly but feel foreign. Your otherworldly knowledge somehow translates into an instinctive understanding of frontier survival and an uncanny ability to read the intentions of dangerous men. The town sheriff eyes you suspiciously as you ride down Main Street.

**Starting Kit:** Horse and saddle, basic western clothing, small amount of silver dollars, holstered revolver  
**Initial Challenges:** Establishing identity and reputation, finding work and shelter, understanding local politics  
**First Contact:** A bartender offers information about local opportunities, or a desperate rancher approaches with a job offer

### B) The Academy - "Deputy Training"

Your consciousness awakens during oath-taking ceremony at the territorial marshal's office, where new deputies are being sworn in to help maintain order across the expanding frontier. Your fellow lawmen are impressed by your tactical thinking and comprehensive understanding of criminal behavior, but they wonder about your unfamiliarity with local customs.

**Starting Kit:** Deputy badge, service revolver, basic law enforcement training, access to wanted posters  
**Initial State:** Probationary lawman with official authority  
**Academy Features:** Gunfighting instruction, tracking and investigation techniques, territorial law education  
**Social Dynamics:** Veteran lawmen testing the newcomer, corrupt officials with hidden agendas, civilians seeking protection

### C) The Guild - "Professional Opportunities"

You find yourself in the back room of the Silver Dollar Saloon, where various frontier professionals conduct business. Your reputation as someone who "gets things done without asking too many questions" has apparently preceded you, and multiple parties are interested in your services—from cattle barons needing security to mining consortiums requiring problem-solving.

**Starting Kit:** Professional reputation, diverse skill set, network of contacts, available work opportunities  
**Guild Options:** Law enforcement (justice and order), Gunslinger services (protection and reputation), Prospecting consortiums (mining and claims), Cattle operations (ranching and driving)  
**Initial Reputation:** Competent professional with unproven local standing  
**First Mission:** Choose between protecting a cattle drive, investigating claim jumping, or tracking down rustlers

### D) The Party - "Posse Formation"

The sheriff's bell clangs urgently through the town as your posse mounts up to pursue the notorious "Black Bart" gang after their latest stagecoach robbery. Your fabricated memories place you as the group's "tracker"—the one who can read sign and understand outlaw psychology well enough to predict where they'll run to ground.

**Starting Kit:** Posse credentials, pursuit supplies, specialized tracking equipment, emergency provisions  
**Team Composition:** Sheriff (leadership and authority), deputy (local knowledge), bounty hunter (combat specialist), local guide (territorial expertise)  
**Current Mission:** Tracking dangerous outlaws through hostile territory while racing against time before they escape to Indian Territory

### E) The Deep End - "Lynching Party Target"

You regain consciousness with a rope around your neck, sitting backwards on a horse beneath a sturdy oak tree while angry townspeople demand justice for a crime you didn't commit. Your unusual knowledge and different mannerisms have made you the perfect scapegoat for recent troubles, but your understanding of frontier psychology might help you talk your way out of this deadly situation.

**Starting Kit:** Restraints, angry mob, false accusations, limited time to prove innocence  
**Immediate Threats:** Lynch mob justice, actual perpetrators still at large, community suspicion  
**Survival Rewards:** Deep understanding of frontier justice and mob psychology, potential allies among those who believe in due process  
**Victory Condition:** Prove innocence and identify real culprits while surviving vigilante justice

### F) The Transplant - "The Spirit of the West"

- A divine spirit of the frontier has brought you here to fulfill a destiny: bring justice to the lawless territories and solve the mystery of the disappearances before it consumes the burgeoning civilization. When your work is done, you'll return home, a hero of a forgotten world.

**Starting Kit:** A reliable D-Tier revolver and horse, 20 silver dollars, and a unique [Manifest Destiny] blessing that grants you uncanny aim in duels and makes law-abiding folk instinctively trust you.
**Initial State:** You have **Friendly (+20)** reputation with any established law enforcement and honest townsfolk. Outlaws and corrupt officials, however, will see you as a major threat to their operations.

### G) The Regression - "The Gunslinger Who Remembers the Ambush"

- You've traveled back in time to prevent the massacre that wiped out your town. Or, you are a veteran of countless Wild West-style dimensions, a legendary figure who has outdrawn the quickest gunslingers across a hundred dusty plains.

**Starting Kit (Time Travel Variant):** You retain enhanced base stats (+5 to AGI/DEX) and skills like `[Quickdraw] Lv. 6` and `[Tracking] Lv. 4`, but your equipment is the simple six-shooter and worn duster your past self owned.
**Starting Kit (Multiversal Hunter Variant):** You arrive with a pair of masterwork, silver-inlaid revolvers (B-Tier), a pouch of 100+ gold coins, and skills like `[Legendary Gunslinger] Lv. 9` and `[Outlaw Network Lore] Lv. 7`. Your [Dimensional Anchor] is a magical saddlebag with infinite storage.
**Initial State:** The local sheriff will wonder how a stranger knows so much about the upcoming outlaw attack. Your challenge is being a savior without being mistaken for a prophet or a demon.

## The Three Territory System

### The Ranchlands - "Where Cattle is King"

**Atmosphere:** Vast rolling plains, sprawling ranch houses, cattle herds stretching to the horizon  
**Population:** Wealthy ranchers, hardworking cowboys, traveling cattle buyers, seasonal workers  
**Economic Base:** Cattle ranching, livestock trading, land speculation, agricultural supplies  
**Threats:** Cattle rustlers, range wars, harsh weather, predatory wildlife

**Key Locations:**

- **Big Sam's Spread:** Massive ranch operation owned by Samuel "Big Sam" Johnson, political and economic powerhouse
- **Stockyards:** Central cattle trading post where deals are made and fortunes won or lost
- **The Cattlemen's Club:** Exclusive saloon where ranch owners discuss business and settle disputes
- **Line Camps:** Remote outposts where cowboys maintain herds and watch for rustlers

**Opportunities:**

- **Cattle Driving:** Seasonal work moving herds to market, dangerous but well-paying
- **Ranch Security:** Protecting cattle operations from rustlers and competing ranchers
- **Land Development:** Acquiring and improving grazing land for expanding operations
- **Political Influence:** Working with ranchers to influence territorial law and development

### The Goldfields - "Where Dreams Come to Die Rich"

**Atmosphere:** Bustling mining camps, tent cities, the constant sound of picks and shovels  
**Population:** Hopeful prospectors, experienced miners, claim speculators, camp followers  
**Economic Base:** Gold prospecting, mining equipment sales, supply services, claim trading  
**Threats:** Claim jumpers, mine collapses, con artists, boom-and-bust economics

**Key Locations:**

- **Goldpan Gus's Claim:** Successful mining operation run by veteran prospector "Goldpan" Gus Thompson
- **The Assay Office:** Where ore is evaluated and claims are registered, center of all mining business
- **Miner's Rest Saloon:** Social hub where prospectors drink, gamble, and share information
- **Equipment Emporium:** Supply store providing tools, provisions, and services to miners

**Unique Features:**

- **Strike Luck:** Random discovery events that can instantly change character fortunes
- **Claim Disputes:** Legal and extralegal conflicts over mining rights and boundaries
- **Boom Town Economics:** Rapidly changing prices and opportunities based on mining success
- **Technological Innovation:** Introduction of new mining techniques and equipment

### The Frontier - "Where the Wild Things Are"

**Atmosphere:** Untamed wilderness, dangerous canyons, mysterious ruins, unexplained phenomena  
**Population:** Trappers, scouts, prospectors seeking undiscovered claims, outlaws hiding from the law  
**Survival Requirements:** Self-sufficiency, wilderness skills, vigilance against natural and human threats  
**Mysteries:** Unexplained disappearances, strange tracks, abandoned settlements, ancient artifacts

**Key Features:**

- **Unexplored Territory:** Vast regions where maps end and adventure begins
- **Natural Hazards:** Wild animals, dangerous weather, treacherous terrain, limited water
- **Outlaw Hideouts:** Remote locations where criminals retreat when the law gets too close
- **Lost Treasures:** Rumors of hidden caches, abandoned mines, and Spanish gold

**Adventure Opportunities:**

- **Exploration Expeditions:** Mapping unknown territory while facing environmental challenges
- **Treasure Hunting:** Following old legends and maps to discover hidden wealth
- **Outlaw Pursuit:** Tracking criminals into their wilderness strongholds
- **Mystery Investigation:** Solving the puzzle of the frontier's unexplained disappearances

## The Gunfighter Reputation System

### Notch-Based Progression

Gunfighter reputation operates on a simple but meaningful progression system that affects social interactions and available opportunities:

| Reputation Level | Notch Count | Social Impact | Opportunities | Challenges |
|-----------------|-------------|---------------|---------------|------------|
| **Tenderfoot** | 0 notches | Unknown, untested | Basic work, no respect | Must prove yourself constantly |
| **First-Notch** | 1 notch | Proven in combat | Local recognition, some trust | Challenges from other gunfighters |
| **Respected Gun** | 2-9 notches | Regional reputation | Leadership opportunities, quality work | Bounty hunters, vengeance seekers |
| **Ten-Notch Legend** | 10+ notches | Legendary status | Political influence, high-paying jobs | Constant challenges, target for fame-seekers |
| **Hundred-Notch Myth** | 100+ notches | Mythical reputation | Regional authority, historical significance | Isolation, few peers, heavy responsibility |

### Reputation Consequences

**Positive Effects:**

- **Intimidation Bonus:** Enemies may surrender rather than fight legendary gunfighters
- **Employment Premium:** Higher pay for protection and enforcement work
- **Political Influence:** Input on local law and territorial development
- **Social Status:** Access to exclusive establishments and social circles

**Negative Effects:**

- **Challenge Magnets:** Young guns seeking to make their reputation by defeating you
- **Bounty Hunter Targets:** Some notches come with prices on your head
- **Isolation:** Few people relate to someone with dozens of kills to their name
- **Moral Weight:** Living with the consequences of a violent profession

## Frontier Professions & Economic Systems

### Primary Career Paths

**Cattle Ranching Operations:**

- **Ranch Owner:** Managing vast cattle operations, land acquisition, political influence
- **Trail Boss:** Leading cattle drives across dangerous territory to market
- **Cowpuncher:** Daily cattle work, riding line, branding, roundups
- **Range Detective:** Investigating cattle theft and protecting ranch interests

**Mining & Prospecting:**

- **Independent Prospector:** Solo mining operations seeking the big strike
- **Mining Consortium Member:** Organized mining with shared resources and risks
- **Claim Speculator:** Buying and selling mining claims for profit
- **Mining Camp Supplier:** Providing equipment, food, and services to miners

**Law Enforcement & Security:**

- **Town Sheriff:** Maintaining order in settled communities
- **Territorial Marshal:** Law enforcement across wider regions
- **Private Security:** Protecting individuals, property, and business interests
- **Bounty Hunter:** Tracking wanted criminals for reward money

**Independent Professionals:**

- **Gunslinger for Hire:** Providing protection and enforcement services
- **Scout and Guide:** Leading expeditions and providing wilderness expertise
- **Gambler:** Making living through cards, dice, and other games of chance
- **Traveling Preacher:** Bringing religion and moral guidance to frontier communities

### Economic Opportunities

**Legitimate Business:**

- **General Store:** Supplying goods to frontier communities
- **Saloon Operations:** Entertainment, accommodation, information hub
- **Transportation:** Stagecoach lines, freight operations, telegraph services
- **Professional Services:** Doctor, lawyer, banker, newspaper editor

**Gray Area Activities:**

- **Claim Jumping:** Illegally taking over abandoned or disputed mining claims
- **Cattle Rustling:** Stealing cattle for resale or personal use
- **Smuggling:** Moving goods across territorial or international borders
- **Protection Rackets:** "Protecting" businesses from threats that may or may not exist

## Frontier Threats & Mysterious Disappearances

### Criminal Organizations

**"Black Bart" Hawkins Gang (B-Tier Outlaw Organization):**

- *Operations:* Stagecoach robbery, bank heists, cattle rustling, intimidation
- *Territory:* Mobile operations across all three regions
- *Leadership:* Bart "Black Bart" Hawkins, ruthless and strategically minded
- *Challenge:* Well-organized, well-armed, familiar with local terrain

**The Rustler Queen's Network (B-Tier Criminal Enterprise):**

- *Leader:* Catherine "The Rustler Queen" O'Reilly, cunning and charismatic
- *Operations:* Sophisticated cattle theft, illegal cattle trading, corruption
- *Methods:* Social manipulation, inside information, carefully planned operations
- *Threat:* Undermines legitimate cattle operations while avoiding direct confrontation

**Spur Fists Enforcement (C-Tier Violence Specialists):**

- *Leader:* Fergus "Spur Fists" O'Sullivan, brutal enforcer
- *Services:* Debt collection, intimidation, elimination of problems
- *Territory:* Available for hire throughout the frontier
- *Reputation:* Feared for ruthless efficiency and lack of mercy

### Environmental & Supernatural Threats

**Wilderness Hazards:**

- **Predatory Wildlife:** Mountain lions, bears, wolves, rattlesnakes
- **Environmental Extremes:** Flash floods, blizzards, drought, extreme heat
- **Geographical Dangers:** Unstable mine shafts, canyon rockslides, quicksand
- **Disease Outbreaks:** Cholera, typhoid, influenza in crowded mining camps

**The Disappearance Mystery:**

- **Pattern Recognition:** Disappearances occur primarily in Frontier territory
- **Victim Demographics:** Lone travelers, small groups, people carrying valuable items
- **Evidence Anomalies:** Sometimes no tracks, sometimes strange tracks, occasional personal items found far from disappearance sites
- **Local Theories:** Indian raids, outlaw activities, wild animals, supernatural causes
- **Investigation Challenges:** Vast territory, limited law enforcement, unreliable witnesses

- *Leadership:* Traveling preachers like Reverend Elijah Smith

- *Community Role:* Social organization, conflict mediation, charitable works
- *Challenge:* Balancing ideals with frontier reality, managing diverse congregations

**Settlement Communities:**

- **Town Councils:** Democratic governance in established settlements
- **Vigilance Committees:** Citizen law enforcement when official law is inadequate
- **Mutual Aid Societies:** Community support for widows, orphans, disaster victims
- **Cultural Organizations:** Preserving traditions and providing social entertainment

### Moral Complexity & Frontier Justice

**Ethical Dilemmas:**

- **Legal vs. Just:** When territorial law conflicts with moral righteousness
- **Individual vs. Community:** Balancing personal goals with community needs
- **Survival vs. Principle:** Making hard choices when ideals conflict with survival
- **Vengeance vs. Justice:** Distinguishing between personal revenge and legitimate punishment

**Community Standards:**

- **Code of the West:** Unwritten rules governing frontier behavior and honor
- **Hospitality Obligations:** Duties to provide aid and shelter to travelers
- **Gender Roles:** Traditional expectations challenged by frontier necessity
- **Racial Relations:** Complex interactions between different ethnic groups on the frontier

## Signature Adventures & Campaign Themes

### Classic Western Scenarios

- **Cattle Drive Protection:** Guarding valuable herds across dangerous territory
- **Mining Claim Disputes:** Resolving conflicts over valuable mineral rights
- **Outlaw Gang Pursuit:** Tracking criminals through hostile wilderness
- **Town Protection:** Defending settlements from organized criminal threats
- **Frontier Exploration:** Mapping unknown territory and facing its challenges

### Major Quest Lines

**"The Great Cattle War":**

Escalating conflicts between major ranchers threaten to turn the Ranchlands into a battlefield. Navigate the complex politics of land, water rights, and cattle ownership while preventing open warfare that could destroy the region's economy.

- *Political Complexity:* Multiple factions with legitimate grievances and competing interests
- *Economic Stakes:* The prosperity of entire communities depends on resolving the conflict
- *Moral Ambiguity:* Both sides have valid points and questionable methods

**"The Lost Mine of Spanish Gold":**

An ancient map leads to a legendary treasure hidden somewhere in the Frontier, but the search attracts dangerous competition and may be connected to the mysterious disappearances plaguing the region.

- *Treasure Hunt Elements:* Following clues, deciphering maps, overcoming obstacles
- *Competitive Pressure:* Multiple parties seeking the same prize
- *Mystery Integration:* Connection to larger frontier mysteries and disappearances

**"The Preacher's Crusade":**

A charismatic religious leader begins consolidating power across frontier communities, but his methods become increasingly authoritarian and his true motives remain questionable.

- *Religious Authority:* Balancing faith with political power and personal ambition
- *Community Impact:* How religious movements affect frontier society and individual freedom
- *Moral Investigation:* Determining whether the preacher represents salvation or oppression

**"The Vanishing Trail":**

Systematic investigation of the frontier disappearances reveals a pattern that suggests something more organized and dangerous than random criminal activity or natural predation.

- *Investigation Challenge:* Gathering evidence across vast, sparsely populated territory
- *Escalating Mystery:* Each clue reveals larger and more disturbing implications
- *Ultimate Revelation:* Discovery of the true cause behind the disappearances

## Achievement System

### Gunfighter Progression

- **First-Notch Tin Horn:** Win your first gunfight, entering the world of professional violence
- **Ten-Notch Legend:** Achieve local legendary status through combat reputation
- **Hundred-Notch Myth:** Reach mythical status as one of the greatest gunfighters in frontier history

### Professional Success

- **Cattle Baron:** Build successful ranching operation with significant regional influence
- **Prospector's Luck:** Discover major gold deposits and achieve mining wealth
- **Law of the Land:** Establish justice and order across significant territory
- **Faith's Servant:** Build church and establish thriving religious community

### Regional Influence

- **Ranchlands Ruler:** Gain respect and influence among the cattle-raising elite
- **Goldfields Guardian:** Protect mining communities from major threats and criminal activity
- **Frontier's Friend:** Earn trust and recognition from the hardy souls living in the untamed wilderness
- **Region's Hero:** Prevent major catastrophe threatening all three territories

### Personal Development

- **True Grit:** Survive extended period in dangerous frontier conditions without outside assistance
- **Silver Tongue:** Build reputation as skilled negotiator and persuasive speaker
- **Treasure Hunter:** Discover significant hidden wealth from frontier's early days
- **Disappearance Detective:** Solve the mystery behind multiple unexplained disappearances

### Frontier Hardships

- **Frontier Survivor:** Turn serious setbacks into character growth and resilience
- **Bullet Wound Badge:** Survive gunfight defeats and learn from the experience
- **Scars of the West:** Demonstrate remarkable resilience through multiple serious challenges

## Romance & Relationships on the Frontier

### Frontier Social Dynamics

- **Scarcity of Women:** Gender imbalance creating competition and social complexity
- **Cross-Class Romance:** Relationships between different economic and social levels
- **Cultural Conflicts:** Romance across ethnic, religious, or cultural boundaries
- **Professional Complications:** Relationships complicated by dangerous frontier professions

### Traditional vs. Progressive

- **Gender Role Expectations:** Traditional expectations challenged by frontier necessity
- **Marriage Arrangements:** Practical marriages vs. romantic love in harsh environment
- **Family Survival:** Relationships focused on mutual survival and community building
- **Independence vs. Partnership:** Balancing individual freedom with relationship commitment

### Frontier-Specific Challenges

- **Geographic Separation:** Relationships strained by vast distances and limited communication
- **Violence and Danger:** Partners facing constant threats from outlaws, wildlife, and environmental hazards
- **Economic Uncertainty:** Relationships affected by boom-and-bust frontier economy
- **Community Pressure:** Small frontier communities creating intense social pressure on relationships

- **Frontier Justice:** Bringing order to lawless territory and protecting innocent people

- **Community Leadership:** Building and guiding frontier communities toward prosperity and safety

### Western Genre Integration

- **Moral Complexity:** Navigating ethical gray areas where right and wrong aren't always clear
