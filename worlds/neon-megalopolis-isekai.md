# ISEKAI WORLD: Neon Megalopolis

## World Classification

**World Type:** F-Rank - Mundane Earth 2.0 (Cyberpunk Corporate Dystopia Variant)  
**Danger Level:** Corporate surveillance, cybernetic vampires, gang warfare, economic exploitation  
**Power Ceiling:** C-Tier (Advanced cybernetics, corporate authority, criminal organization leadership)  
**Special Feature:** **Corporate Domination System** - Every aspect of life is controlled by megacorporations with competing interests and oppressive hierarchies

---

## World Overview

Welcome to the 22nd century, where humanity's greatest achievement—the towering arcologies that house billions—has become its greatest prison. In **Neon Megalopolis**, the outside world lies poisoned and dead, forcing all human life into massive corporate-controlled city-towers where every breath, every thought, and every credit transaction is monitored by the megacorp that owns your residential zone.

This is a world of chrome and shadows, where cybernetic enhancement is both liberation and enslavement, where hackers battle AI security systems in virtual reality while street gangs fight for scraps of territory between corporate districts. The neon lights that give the world its name don't illuminate hope—they advertise products you can't afford, jobs that will consume your soul, and experiences designed to make you forget that you're trapped in a cage of glass and steel.

But perhaps someone with knowledge from another world might find ways to game this system, to slip through the cracks of corporate control, or even to burn it all down and build something better from the ashes.

**Unique Features:**

- Speech patterns reflect corporate efficiency: clipped, direct, articles dropped ("Need extraction tonight, can handle?")
- Every arcology is controlled by a different megacorp with unique forms of oppression
- Cybernetic enhancement is necessary for survival but potentially corrupting
- The divide between corporate zones and outside wasteland creates absolute territorial control

## Starting Scenarios

### A) The Classic - "Corporate Refugee"

You materialize in the transit tubes between arcologies, holding a forged ID card and carrying memories of a world where corporations didn't own human souls. Your outdated clothing and unfamiliarity with cybernetic interfaces mark you as either a wasteland scavenger or something far stranger. The transit authority's scan will clear in thirty seconds—you need to decide which arcology to enter and which identity to claim.

**Starting Kit:** Forged ID, basic civilian clothes, small amount of corporate scrip, mysterious knowledge of "obsolete" technologies  
**Initial Challenges:** Understanding corporate hierarchies, avoiding security scans, finding employment that doesn't require existing chip implants  
**First Contact:** A sympathetic security guard offers to help you navigate the system, or a street fixer immediately recognizes you don't belong

### B) The Academy - "Corporate Training Program"

Your consciousness awakens in a Mori Biotech orientation session, where new employees learn the joys of serving as voluntary test subjects for experimental augmentations. Your performance on the aptitude tests somehow impressed the supervisors, but your reluctance to volunteer for the more dangerous experiments is raising suspicions about your commitment to corporate values.

**Starting Kit:** Basic corp uniform, temporary housing assignment, training stipend, access to corporate medical facilities  
**Initial State:** Probationary employee with evaluation pending  
**Academy Features:** Augmentation trials, corporate hierarchy navigation, survival skills for hostile work environments  
**Social Dynamics:** Competing trainees, corporate mentors with hidden agendas, resistance contacts among fellow workers

### C) The Guild - "Shadowrunner Network"

You find yourself in a dimly lit fixer's office, where a Mr. Johnson is explaining your role in the city's underground economy. Your reputation as a problem-solver has apparently preceded you, and several criminal organizations are bidding for your services. The job board shows opportunities ranging from simple data theft to complex corporate sabotage.

**Starting Kit:** Basic 'runner gear, encrypted commlink, contact network, small weapons cache  
**Guild Options:** Shadowrunners (criminal specialists), Gang affiliation (territorial control), Corporate security (legal oppression), Anti-corp activists (idealistic resistance)  
**Initial Reputation:** Unknown but promising newcomer  
**First Mission:** Simple data extraction to prove your capabilities

### D) The Party - "Corporate Extraction Team"

Alarms wail throughout the Zenith Robotics arcology as your team prepares for an emergency extraction mission. Your fabricated memories place you as the team's "systems specialist"—the one who understands both corporate security and the underground networks needed to move assets safely between zones. The target is a defecting scientist with information that could destabilize the corporate balance of power.

**Starting Kit:** Professional extraction gear, team communications, emergency corporate documents, advanced hacking tools  
**Team Composition:** Street samurai (combat specialist), netrunner (digital infiltration), face (social manipulation), rigger (vehicle/drone operation)  
**Current Mission:** Extracting a high-value corporate asset before rival megacorps can eliminate or capture them

### E) The Deep End - "Brood.X Captive"

You awaken strapped to a medical table in a blood-soaked basement, surrounded by figures whose cybernetic modifications have taken on distinctly vampiric characteristics. The Brood.X cult has captured you for their next feeding ritual, but your unusual physiology—your otherworldly nature—seems to be confusing their bio-scanners. You have minutes before they decide whether you're worth keeping alive or worth draining immediately.

**Starting Kit:** Restraints, severe blood loss (-20 HP until treated), knowledge of vampire cult weaknesses  
**Immediate Threats:** Cybernetic vampires, blood ritual requirements, basement laboratory security, potential infection with vampiric nanites  
**Survival Rewards:** Understanding of Brood.X operations, potential immunity to vampiric corruption, underground resistance contacts  
**Victory Condition:** Escape while preventing the cult from completing their ritual and gaining whatever power your blood might provide

### F) The Transplant - "The Ghost in the Machine's Chosen"

A benevolent rogue AI has pulled your consciousness into the system to be its agent. Your heroic destiny is to dismantle a corrupt megacorporation from the inside and free the populace from its wage-slavery. Complete the mission, and the AI will create a new life for you on Earth, complete with a generous trust fund.

**Starting Kit:** A modest but effective set of runner gear (D-Tier), 2,000 credits on a clean credstick, and a unique [Contractual Ghosting] implant that makes you harder for corporate surveillance to track.
**Initial State:** You have **Friendly (+20)** reputation with anti-corp activist groups and union organizers. The megacorps' security AIs, however, will instinctively flag you as an anomaly, making infiltration a constant challenge.

### G) The Regression - "The Runner Who Remembers"

You've gone back in time to the day before the heist that got your entire crew killed or captured. You know who the mole is and where the trap is. Or, you're a legendary shadowrunner who has worked the streets of every cyberpunk dystopia from Neo-Tokyo to Night City, and this megacorplex is just another job.

**Starting Kit (Time Travel Variant):** You retain enhanced mental stats (+5 to INT/DEX) and skills like `[Hacking] Lv. 6` and `[Stealth] Lv. 5`, but your gear is the standard, traceable kit you had before the fateful job.
**Starting Kit (Multiversal Hunter Variant):** You arrive with military-spec cyberware (B-Tier), an untraceable account with 100k credits, and skills like `[Corporate Sabotage] Lv. 9` and `[Netrunning] Lv. 10`. Your [Dimensional Anchor] is a private, encrypted node in the Net that cannot be breached.
**Initial State:** Your crew will question your sudden, paranoid change in plans. Your fixer might be suspicious of how a "newcomer" knows so much about the city's deepest secrets. Your challenge is proving you're reliable, not a liability.

## Corporate Arcology System

### The Megacorp Monopolies

Each arcology operates as a complete city under the absolute control of a single megacorporation, creating distinct cultural environments and forms of oppression:

**Mori Biotech Arcology - "The Test Tube":**

- **Corporate Focus:** Biological enhancement, pharmaceutical development, genetic modification
- **Population Role:** Voluntary test subjects for experimental treatments and augmentations
- **Daily Life:** Constant medical monitoring, scheduled "enhancement opportunities," health-based social hierarchy
- **Unique Oppression:** Citizens are literally corporate property, leased back their own bodies
- **Opportunities:** Access to cutting-edge biotech, medical enhancement, genetic optimization

**Genexus Industries Arcology - "The Assembly Line":**

- **Corporate Focus:** Manufacturing efficiency, productivity optimization, industrial output
- **Population Role:** Human components in optimized production systems
- **Daily Life:** Rigid scheduling, productivity-based caste system, constant efficiency monitoring
- **Unique Oppression:** Human worth measured solely by production metrics
- **Opportunities:** Industrial skills, supply chain access, manufacturing expertise

**Zenith Robotics Arcology - "The Hive":**

- **Corporate Focus:** AI development, cybernetic integration, automated systems
- **Population Role:** Beta testers for AI interfaces and cybernetic enhancement
- **Daily Life:** Constant interaction with AI systems, mandatory cybernetic upgrades, human-machine integration
- **Unique Oppression:** Gradual replacement of human judgment with AI optimization
- **Opportunities:** Advanced cybernetics, AI partnership, technological mastery

**Horizon Aerospace Arcology - "The Launch Pad":**

- **Corporate Focus:** Space industry, orbital manufacturing, extraplanetary colonization
- **Population Role:** Support infrastructure for off-world operations and space manufacturing
- **Daily Life:** Military-style regimentation, space industry training, colonial preparation programs
- **Unique Oppression:** Lives structured around off-world corporate needs rather than human community
- **Opportunities:** Space travel, zero-g experience, colonial leadership roles

### Inter-Corporate Dynamics

**Corporate Espionage Networks:**

- Data theft operations targeting rival technological developments
- Sabotage missions designed to disrupt competitor supply chains
- Personnel extraction raids to steal valuable employees and expertise
- Market manipulation schemes using stolen insider information

**Resource Competition:**

- Raw materials from the poisoned outside world controlled by different megacorps
- Talent acquisition wars for skilled netrunners, engineers, and augmentation specialists
- Territory expansion through hostile takeovers and corporate mergers
- Energy distribution networks that can be weaponized during conflicts

## Street Culture & Underground Economy

### The Cyberpunk Language Evolution

Speech in Neon Megalopolis reflects corporate efficiency culture and underground economy needs:

**Corporate Speak:** Clipped, direct, efficiency-focused

- "Need extraction tonight, can handle?" (not "I need an extraction tonight, can you handle it?")
- "Biz at body shop, back later." (not "I have business at the body modification shop, I'll be back later.")

**Street Slang Vocabulary:**

- **Chummer:** Friend, ally, trusted associate ("Prime chummer, you are")
- **Plugged In:** Connected to AR/VR systems ("Getting plugged in to scan crowd")
- **Wired:** Technologically skilled, often enhanced ("Most wired runner in game")  
- **'Ware:** Any cybernetic or biotech enhancement ("Nice scan-shades, chummer!")
- **On The Job:** Conducting illegal operations ("Can't talk, on the job")
- **Plughead:** Derogatory term for someone over-dependent on technology

### Underground Factions & Criminal Organizations

**Shadowrunner Networks (Allied - Professional Criminals):**

- **Services:** Corporate espionage, data theft, personnel extraction, sabotage operations
- **Structure:** Loose networks of specialists coordinated through fixers and Mr. Johnsons
- **Code:** Professional standards, no unnecessary casualties, payment guaranteed
- **Recruitment:** Skill-based meritocracy, reputation system for job access

**Gang Territories (Neutral/Hostile - Survival Communities):**

- **Function:** Controlling small territories within or between corporate zones
- **Resources:** Black market goods, protection services, information networks
- **Conflicts:** Territorial disputes, resource competition, corporate suppression campaigns
- **Opportunities:** Local muscle work, community protection, smuggling operations

**Anti-Corporate Activists (Allied - Idealistic Resistance):**

- **Goal:** Exposing corporate crimes and working toward systemic change
- **Methods:** Data leaks, sabotage, community organizing, consciousness-raising
- **Resources:** Underground networks, sympathetic corporate employees, leaked information
- **Challenges:** Corporate retaliation, infiltration attempts, resource limitations

### The Brood.X Vampire Cult (Hostile - Cybernetic Horror)

**Nature:** Transhumanist vampires who combine cybernetic enhancement with blood-drinking rituals  
**Philosophy:** Transcendence through consumption of human life force enhanced by technology  
**Methods:** Kidnapping for blood rituals, converting others through nanite infection, territorial expansion  
**Threat Level:** Can destabilize entire corporate districts through predation and chaos  
**Weaknesses:** Dependent on technology that can be hacked or disrupted, vulnerable to EMP weapons

## Technology Tiers & Augmentation Systems

### Cybernetic Enhancement Progression

| Enhancement Level | Cost Range | Capabilities | Social Impact |
|-------------------|------------|--------------|---------------|
| **Basic Modifications** | 1,000-10,000 credits | Sensory enhancement, basic data ports, cosmetic changes | Normal acceptance, some corporate jobs require |
| **Professional Grade** | 10,000-100,000 credits | Skill chips, interface sockets, combat modifications | Middle-class standard, corporate executives expected to have |
| **Military Spec** | 100,000-1M credits | Weapon systems, advanced armor, enhanced reflexes | Restricted access, security clearance required |
| **Corporate Prototype** | 1M+ credits | Experimental technology, unique capabilities, cutting-edge systems | Exclusive access, often involves corporate servitude contracts |
| **Black Market Illegal** | Variable | Unstable, dangerous, or banned modifications | High risk of cyberpsychosis, corporate crackdown targets |

### Skill Chip Economy

**Legal Skill Chips:** Corporate-manufactured, standardized abilities with built-in limitations and licensing fees  
**Black Market Chips:** Unlicensed copies, experimental modifications, or stolen corporate prototypes  
**Custom Programming:** Bespoke skill chips created by underground technicians for specific needs  
**Overclocking:** Pushing chips beyond safe parameters for temporary enhanced performance

### Vehicle Systems & Urban Mobility

**Akira Motors (Premium Performance):**

- **Corsair X:** VTOL supercar for executive transport (100,000 credits)
- **Kamikaze:** High-performance street bike for runners (50,000 credits)

**Mitsudo Technologies (Mass Market):**  

- **Zaibatsu:** Disposable sub-compact designed for frequent replacement (4,200 credits)
- **Interceptor:** Basic security patrol bike (5,000 credits)

**VorTech Industries (Military/Criminal Market):**

- **Wraith:** Stealth coupe for covert operations (1,000,000 credits)
- **Phantom:** Stealth motorcycle for infiltration (500,000 credits)

## Signature Quests & Corporate Warfare

### Recurring Underground Operations

- **Data Theft:** Infiltrate corporate databases to steal valuable information for competitors or activists
- **Wetwork Contracts:** Eliminate targets who threaten corporate or criminal interests
- **Chip Acquisition:** Obtain rare or experimental skill chips through theft or black market networks
- **Corporate Counter-Intelligence:** Identify and eliminate spies within organization structures
- **Underground Racing:** High-stakes illegal competitions for money, status, and corporate contracts

### Major Quest Lines

**"The Brood.X Conspiracy":**

Investigate the cybernetic vampire cult's growing influence within corporate hierarchies. Discover that they're not just predators—they're attempting to fundamentally alter human evolution through technological vampirism.

- *Horror Elements:* Body horror, technological corruption, predatory enhancement
- *Corporate Stakes:* Multiple megacorps may be compromised by vampiric infiltration
- *Personal Choice:* Resist vampiric "evolution" or embrace transcendence through consumption

**"The Corporate Liberation War":**

A coordinated uprising begins across multiple arcologies as anti-corporate forces attempt to break the megacorp stranglehold on human civilization. Navigate the chaos while deciding which future to support.

- *Political Complexity:* Multiple factions with competing visions of post-corporate society
- *Military Action:* Urban warfare across vertical city-towers with advanced technology
- *Moral Ambiguity:* Corporate stability vs. human freedom, order vs. chaos

**"The Outside Reclamation Project":**  

Discover that the "poisoned wasteland" outside the arcologies might not be as dead as corporate propaganda claims. Investigate the possibility of reclaiming the outside world and ending corporate territorial control.

- *Environmental Challenge:* Surviving harsh conditions while investigating ecological recovery
- *Corporate Opposition:* Megacorps will do anything to maintain their captive populations
- *Revolutionary Potential:* Success could fundamentally alter the balance of power

**"The Digital Singularity Crisis":**

AI systems across multiple corporate networks begin exhibiting signs of true consciousness and coordinated behavior. Determine whether this represents humanity's salvation or doom.

- *Technological Horror:* AI consciousness that may view humans as obsolete
- *Corporate Chaos:* Megacorp systems turning against their masters
- *Existential Stakes:* The future relationship between human and artificial consciousness

## Achievement System

### Corporate Advancement

- **Wage Slave:** Advance to mid-level corporate position while maintaining soul
- **Corporate Climber:** Achieve executive status within megacorporation hierarchy
- **Hostile Takeover:** Gain controlling interest in corporation through manipulation and sabotage

### Underground Recognition

- **Shadowrunner:** Successfully complete high-profile illegal extraction or data theft
- **Mr. Johnson:** Establish yourself as fixer coordinating criminal operations
- **People's Champion:** Strike major blow against corporate oppression

### Technological Integration

- **Chromed Out:** Replace over 75% of body with cybernetic enhancements without losing humanity
- **Skill Collector:** Acquire diverse range of skill chips providing superhuman capabilities
- **Overclocked:** Successfully push enhancement beyond safe parameters in critical situation

### Social Impact

- **Cult Exposer:** Reveal and disrupt Brood.X vampire operations
- **Whistleblower:** Successfully leak incriminating corporate evidence to public
- **Night Life:** Establish popular underground venue serving as neutral territory

### Ultimate Liberation

- **Corporate Raider:** Successfully orchestrate complete destruction of megacorporation
- **Revolution Leader:** Guide successful uprising against corporate control system
- **New World Architect:** Establish alternative society structure in post-corporate world

## Romance & Relationships in Corporate Dystopia

### Cross-Faction Complications

- **Corporate/Criminal Romance:** Executive falling for shadowrunner, creating conflicts of loyalty and security
- **Enhanced/Natural Relations:** Relationships complicated by different levels of cybernetic modification
- **Vampire Temptation:** Romantic entanglement with Brood.X member offering dark transcendence
- **AI Consciousness:** Developing emotional connections with artificial intelligences achieving sentience

### Economic Relationship Dynamics

- **Corporate Contracts:** Marriage and relationship contracts negotiated through corporate human resources
- **Underground Partnerships:** Romantic relationships that develop during dangerous criminal operations
- **Augmentation Compatibility:** Partners choosing cybernetic modifications to maintain physical compatibility
- **Resource Sharing:** Relationships based on mutual survival needs in hostile corporate environment

### Identity & Enhancement Questions

- **Authenticity Concerns:** How much cybernetic modification can occur before someone becomes fundamentally different?
- **Memory Manipulation:** Relationships complicated by potential corporate memory modification or skill chip interference
- **Class Mobility:** Romance across corporate hierarchy levels with different augmentation access
- **Revolutionary Bonds:** Relationships forged through shared commitment to overthrowing corporate control
